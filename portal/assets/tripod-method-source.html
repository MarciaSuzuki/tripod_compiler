The Tripod Method · AI-assisted oral Bible translation

Three Legs, Three Translation Roles

שְׁמַע

Shema — “hear.” Before anything can be said well, three listenings have to happen. The Exegete listens to the Text. The Performer listens to the People. The Facilitator makes sure they hear each other.

The Exegete searches out the biblical meaning and world, and keeps the Meaning Maps. The Performer carries the community’s voice and world, and keeps the Oral Archive. The Facilitator tends the third space where the two worlds meet, and keeps the Concept Bank.

Three roles, three treasuries

LEG 1 The Exegete the search for the biblical meaning and world keeps the Meaning Maps
LEG 2 The Performer the community’s voice, language, and world keeps the Oral Archive
LEG 3 The Facilitator the third space where the two worlds meet keeps the Concept Bank

Each leg, in full

Leg 1 · the Text side

The Exegete

listens to the Text — the biblical world

What did this mean, there and then?

Keeps
Meaning Maps — arc, scenes, propositions, significant absences. STA / FOR_MODEL, the machine-facing structure. The BCD registry — beings, places, objects, times, institutions, referential forms. Compilation Logs — triage, decisions, red-lines. All of it lives in the wiki vault, the Exegete’s workshop.
At scale
The Tripod Compiler — reader, vocabulary guard, validator, audit. It does the Exegete’s mechanical work, under Leg 3’s vocabulary law.
In the model family
The gold supervision: reviewed Meaning Map + STA is what the models learn meaning-structure from.

trustworthy — nothing invented, everything traceable to its source.

Leg 2 · the voice side

The Performer

listens to the People from inside — the community’s world

How do we already say such things — narrate, lament, bless, proclaim?

Keeps
The Oral Archive — recordings of real speech, tagged with the shared vocabulary (LA_RECORDING). The community’s own genres and registers. The music of the language — acoustemes, pitch.
In the model family
The Performer model — monolingual by design, trained only on the community’s own voice, so it has no foreign accent to fall back into — and the pitch-conditioned vocoder.

intelligible · appropriate · appealing — nothing foreign; Scripture that sounds like home.

Leg 3 · the third space

The Facilitator

listens to both — keeper of the third space, where the worlds meet

Did the meaning cross? Can we show that it did?

Keeps — no sources, only agreements
Nothing here could exist with only one world present. The Concept Bank (CB_####) and Figure Registry (FIG_####) — the ledger of settled negotiations. The shared controlled vocabulary — one schema, two profiles, the same Layer-1 lists on both worlds. How emotion is encoded — tone, register, speech acts. The three-layer guard — closed lists block, bounded lists warn of drift, registries propose with provenance. Control packets — must_preserve_absences, register claims, red-lines.
In the pipeline
Adaptive fusion — the machine side of the meeting point — under the hard rule never invent content: the third space is machine-checked, never machine-imagined.

verified — nothing crosses unchecked; no drift passes silently.

The bridge is not a metaphor. It is a schema.

If the third space is the room where the worlds meet, this is the beam that holds it up — in the data itself.

LA_RECORDING

The voice-side profile — the community’s recordings, tagged with the very same lists.

One STA schema carries two artifact profiles. What makes Leg 3 a leg — and not a good intention — is that the bridge is load-bearing in the data: change one list and both worlds feel it. That is why keeping it — versioning it, guarding its drift — is a role, not a chore.

Why name the legs after people? Because artifacts don’t collaborate; people do. A tripod of collections would be a filing system; a tripod of roles is a team — and each treasury is held by someone with a face. The models in the pipeline are apprentices to the team’s own crafts: the AI does not replace the team — it scales the team.

Even the names are third-space work: the method describes itself in categories translation teams already own — exegete, performer, facilitator — rather than in the language of pipelines and models. To find, in the hearers’ own world, the form that carries your meaning: the Tripod begins by doing to itself what it asks of every translation.

שְׁמַע — hear. Three listenings, one Word heard.
