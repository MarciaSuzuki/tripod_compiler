## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Burden / Concern
The passage opens the fable by setting a place and a people, and showing them at peace with their own nature. In the middle of the vast Pacific there is an island ringed by clear blue water, full of bright coral reefs. A cheerful shoal of little fish lives there. They spend their days swimming back and forth, exploring the beauty below and diving into the hidden caves of the coral. Swimming is their life. They do it naturally, without ever thinking about it. What weighs on the passage is exactly that last line: the fish possess their defining ability so completely that it is invisible to them. The arc is a baseline being laid down — a state of unselfconscious belonging — and the burden it carries is that this very invisibility is what the rest of the story will exploit. The concern planted here is the gap between what a creature *is* and what it *notices about itself*.

### 2.2 Context
This is the opening of the fable. No era is named; the time is the timeless present of a told story. The place is established with care: a single island in the open Pacific, surrounded by coral. The "people" are a shoal of small fish, treated throughout as a community with a shared way of life. The audience is expected to know, from the start, that fish swim — and the passage leans on that shared knowledge, since its whole point is that the fish themselves will come to forget it. Nothing in this passage signals the reversal to come; it reads as a simple, contented beginning, which is what makes it work as a setup.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The tone is warm and untroubled. The pace is gentle and even — long, unhurried sentences that move the way the fish move, back and forth, with no tension and no event. There is no conflict yet, no single actor, no problem. The mood is settled contentment. The only faint weight is in the closing clause — *without ever thinking about it* — which a listener feels as ordinary now but which the story will later turn into the hinge of its irony. The pace neither rushes nor drags; it simply rests in a stable world before anything happens to it.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
The passage opens the story by establishing the world, the community, and their nature, and by planting the one fact the whole fable depends on: that swimming is so natural to the fish that they do not think about it. To speak this passage is to set in place the island, the water, the shoal, their daily life among the coral, and above all their unselfconscious ease in their own element. This is groundwork. It introduces no problem and resolves nothing; its work is to make the audience feel the baseline so the later forgetting will register as a loss. Its mode is gentle and scene-setting, but it is already quietly ironic in its final note — naming, as a simple fact, the very self-knowledge the fish will lose.
