# P03 — Ruth 1:15–18

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** Naomi's last appeal; Ruth's vow; Naomi's silence
- **BCV:** Ruth 1:15–18
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
The whole passage sits in INFORMAL_CASUAL. Scenes 1 and 2 shift to INTIMATE — a mother-in-law and her daughter-in-law speaking face to face on the road, with no crowd listening and no formal setting. Even Ruth's vow at v.16-17 stays INTIMATE rather than ceremonial: the oath at v.17b is binding and weighty, but that weight is carried by the figures and the deeper encoding, not by a grander way of speaking. Scene 3 goes back to the plain narrator key for the report of Naomi seeing Ruth's resolve and falling silent.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage moves from Naomi's last try to send Ruth away, through Ruth's binding herself to Naomi for good, to Naomi's silence. Naomi points to Orpah, who has gone back, as the example Ruth should follow. Ruth answers with one long speech that ties herself to Naomi in six steps and seals it with an oath calling down a curse on herself if she breaks it. Naomi sees how set Ruth is, and stops speaking. What carries the passage is the turn from being torn apart to being bound together in a way that cannot be undone — all of it done inside a single speech. The question it leaves is whether Ruth's spoken bond to Naomi — to her people, to her God, to the very ground she will die on — will hold through everything the rest of the book brings.

### 2.2 Context
This picks up the moment after Orpah turns back at 1:14. The same road from Moab toward Judah runs on. Naomi has already tried twice in P02 to send the daughters-in-law home, and has watched Orpah go. Here she tries one last time, and Ruth gives her answer. Four threads the book has been pulling since the start all come together in this vow: loyal kindness answered with loyal kindness; a Moabite outsider being taken in; God quietly at work; and Naomi's search for a safe, settled home.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
Close-up and slow. After P02's nine quick verses, this one is just four, and most of it is given to a single long speech. As Ruth's vow climbs — from walking together, to lodging together, to dying and being buried together — the feeling rises step by step, yet it never leaves the warmth of family talk. The ending sits unsettled: Naomi sees, Naomi falls silent, and the narrator leaves it there, with nothing resolved.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
This passage sets down Ruth's binding herself to Naomi as her own free choice, spoken out loud, before the story has put her in any formal place. Her silent holding-on at 1:14 was the first half of how she joins herself to Naomi's people; this vow is the second half, now in words. It is the first time a Moabite outsider ties herself to Israel's people and Israel's God, and the first time God's name is on Ruth's lips. To speak this passage is to hand on a binding promise made with no altar, no court, no gathered community — chosen on a road, by a foreign widow, with no one to witness it but her mother-in-law.

### Scene 2 — Ruth's vow (v.16–17)

**3A — Beings**
[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: the speaker; binds herself to Naomi in one long speech
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; here she speaks at length for the first time in the book
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: the one Ruth speaks to all through the vow
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth; the one Ruth will not leave
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B10-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: the God Ruth calls on in her oath to hold her to it
- Relationship: named by Ruth at v.17 as the one who will enforce the oath; the first time God's name is on Ruth's lips
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B31-People-of-YHWH]] — עַמֵּךְ / "your people" (the people of Israel)
- Role: the people Ruth makes her own at v.16 ("your people will be my people")
- Relationship: Naomi's people, now bound to Ruth by her own word
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
בַּדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continued)
- Role: where Ruth makes her vow
- Type: a road they are traveling
- Meaning: the same road, on from Scene 1
- Effect on scene: Ruth's promise is sealed on the road itself, not at any place they have reached

אֲשֶׁר / "where" / "wherever" (four open "where" phrases inside the vow)
- Role: the four open places Ruth names as she binds herself, one after another
- Type: a place left open on purpose — "wherever," with no name
- Meaning: where you go / where you lodge / where you die / there I will be buried — Ruth ties herself to wherever Naomi's road leads, without naming a single actual place
- Effect on scene: the four open "wheres" keep the promise wide open; no place-code is given

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B9-Ruth]] Ruth answers [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi with one continuous speech. She first refuses Naomi's directive to turn back. She then binds herself to Naomi in six successive vows: wherever Naomi walks, Ruth will walk; wherever Naomi lodges, Ruth will lodge; Naomi's people will be Ruth's people; Naomi's God will be Ruth's God; wherever Naomi dies, Ruth will die; where Naomi is buried, Ruth will be buried. She closes with an oath, calling down a curse on herself and naming [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH out loud: may YHWH do so to her, and worse, if anything but death itself parts her from Naomi.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Records Ruth binding herself to Naomi as her own free, spoken choice — rising step by step from where they walk, to where they lodge, to whose people and whose God will be hers, to where they die and are buried — and sealed with an oath that calls down a curse on herself and names YHWH out loud.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator never calls Ruth "the Moabite" anywhere in this scene. At the very moment she binds herself, he does not undercut it by drawing the foreign line again. Ruth names no place inside the vow — not Bethlehem, not anywhere; every place she ties herself to is left open, a "wherever." She says "your God" at v.16 without naming him; God's name comes only in the oath at v.17. And she gives no reasons for the bond beyond the bond itself.

### Scene 3 — Naomi's silence (v.18)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: seeing-subject who falls silent
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: the one Naomi watches; set in her resolve, and silent now
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Presence: PRESENT

**3B — Places**
בַּדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continued)
- Role: where the silence falls
- Type: a road they are traveling
- Meaning: the same road, on from Scenes 1 and 2
- Effect on scene: the two are left together, still on the road, the moment Naomi stops speaking

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi sees that [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth is determined to go with her, and she stops speaking to her.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Marks the failure of Naomi's last try to send Ruth away, and the moment from which the two go on together. Ends the talk on the road.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator tells us Naomi sees and Naomi stops speaking, but nothing of what is going on inside her. He reports no agreement, no blessing, no setting off again, no acceptance, no further word from Naomi at all in this passage. What her silence after Ruth's vow means is left open, and the passage ends right there.
