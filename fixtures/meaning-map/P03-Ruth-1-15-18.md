# P03 — Ruth 1:15–18

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** Naomi's last appeal; Ruth's vow; Naomi's silence
- **BCV:** Ruth 1:15–18
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
Pericope-level default is INFORMAL_CASUAL, the unmarked default register for biblical narrator voice. Scenes 1 and 2 carry scene-level INTIMATE — mother-in-law and daughter-in-law speaking face-to-face on the road, no community audience and no formal covenant venue. INTIMATE fits the social setting better than CEREMONIAL even for Ruth's vow at v.16-17, despite the institutional weight of the oath at v.17b; the binding-force of the oath is carried by the figure flags and by downstream STA encoding, not by elevated register. Scene 3 returns to the INFORMAL_CASUAL narrator default for the narrator's report of Naomi seeing and stopping speech. CONSULTATIVE is not used for narrator voice (hard rule).

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The pericope moves from Naomi's release-attempt through Ruth's covenant-binding to Naomi's silence. Naomi names Orpah's return-trajectory as the example Ruth should follow; Ruth answers with a six-step binding ladder closed by a self-curse oath; Naomi sees Ruth's resolve and stops speaking. The burden is the shift from severance to indissoluble binding, sealed inside one extended speech. The concern is whether Ruth's verbal binding to Naomi — to her people, to her God, to her death-place — will hold across what the rest of the book brings.

### 2.2 Context
The pericope picks up immediately after Orpah's exit at 1:14 (P02 P15). Same road from Moab toward the land of Judah continues. Naomi has just released the daughters-in-law twice in P02 and watched Orpah accept the release. This pericope is her last attempt and Ruth's structural answer. The four book-spanning discourse threads opened across P01-P02 (T4 hesed-answered-with-hesed, T5 Moabite-outsider-incorporation, T6 YHWH-at-work, plus T1 Naomi-security-and-rest still active) all intersect at the vow.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
Intimate and slowed. The pace narrows from P02's nine-verse density to four-verse weight, with most of the pericope held by one extended speech. The tone rises across the binding ladder from movement to mortality without ever leaving the kinship register. The closing mood is unsettled: Naomi sees, Naomi stops speaking, but the narrator gives no resolution.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
Records Ruth's covenant-binding to Naomi as her own free verbal act, before the narrative has placed her in any institutional frame. Marks the silent dabaq-cling at 1:14 as the first half of Ruth's incorporation and the vow as the verbal second half. Establishes the first explicit Moabite-outsider self-binding to Israel's people and Israel's God, and the first time the divine name appears on Ruth's lips. Speaking the passage transmits a covenant act done outside any altar, court, or community — chosen on a road, by a foreign widow, with no witness but her mother-in-law.

[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: mother-in-law making a last appeal
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth and [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah; widow of [[B2-Elimelech]] Elimelech
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: addressee of Naomi's appeal
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; Moabite by birth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B8-Orpah]] — עָרְפָּה / Orpah
- Role: exemplar Naomi points to; named only by kinship form in this pericope
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; co-daughter-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth (sister-in-law in the levirate sense at v.15)
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
בַּדֶּרֶךְ / the road (implied; continues from P02)
- Role: setting of Naomi's last appeal
- Type: journey locus
- Meaning: the road from Moab toward the land of Judah
- Effect on scene: keeps the speakers in motion-state while Naomi stops them to speak

[[PL2-Fields-of-Moab]] — שָׂדֶה מוֹאָב / fields of Moab (referenced)
- Role: place toward which Orpah has gone back
- Type: foreign land
- Meaning: the territory now associated with Orpah's return
- Effect on scene: identifies the trajectory Naomi names for Ruth to follow

**3C — Objects and Elements**
הִנֵּה / "look, behold" — directive presentational marker
- What it is: speech-opening attention marker pointing the hearer to an exemplar
- Function in scene: directs Ruth's attention to Orpah's act as the pattern to follow
- Signals: presentational force opening the appeal

יְבִמְתֵּךְ / yebimtekh — "your sister-in-law" (kinship form for Orpah)
- What it is: the levirate-relevant kinship term Naomi uses for Orpah; specifically "your husband's brother's wife"
- Function in scene: names Orpah by kinship-relation only; Orpah's personal name is not used in P03
- Signals: continues the exit-finality kinship-only naming established at 1:14 (P02 P15); cross-occurs at v.15 second clause
- Cross-ref: [[CB_0019-Yebimtekh-Sister-in-law]]

עַמָּהּ / "her people" (Moabite collective)
- What it is: referenced collective; Orpah's return-destination at the people-level
- Function in scene: identifies one half of Orpah's return-trajectory
- Signals: no B-code; structural counterpart to "your people" at v.16

אֱלֹהֶיהָ / "her gods" (Moabite deities)
- What it is: referenced collective; Orpah's return-destination at the deities-level
- Function in scene: identifies the other half of Orpah's return-trajectory
- Signals: no B-code; unnamed; structural counterpart to "YHWH" named at v.17

שׁוּבִי אַחֲרֵי יְבִמְתֵּךְ / "go back, after your sister-in-law" — directive with example-pointing rationale
- What it is: directive verb plus follow-the-exemplar clause
- Function in scene: closes Naomi's appeal with the directive proper
- Signals: example-pointing logic — go because your sister-in-law has gone

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey established in P02.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi speaks to [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth: she points to [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah (named only by the kinship form yebimtekh) having returned to her people and her gods, and directs Ruth to go back after her.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Establishes Naomi's final release-attempt and presents Orpah's return as the example Ruth should follow.

**Significant Absence**
Naomi does not name Moab as a place; she names only "her people" and "her gods." Naomi does not name Moabite gods. The unnamed-gods at v.15 stands in structural contrast with YHWH named at v.17.

### Scene 2 — Ruth's vow (v.16–17)

**3A — Beings**
[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: speaking subject; binds herself to Naomi in extended speech
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; speaks now as her own voice for the first extended speech in the book
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: addressee throughout Ruth's vow
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth; the one Ruth refuses to leave
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B10-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: invoked divine agent in the self-curse oath formula
- Relationship: named by Ruth at v.17 as the enforcer of the oath; first time YHWH appears on Ruth's lips
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B31-People-of-YHWH]] — עַמֵּךְ / "your people" (Israelite covenant people)
- Role: collective Ruth binds herself to in the people-binding at v.16
- Relationship: referenced as Naomi's people, now bound to Ruth by speech
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
בַּדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continued)
- Role: setting of Ruth's vow
- Type: journey locus
- Meaning: the road continues from Scene 1
- Effect on scene: keeps Ruth's binding in motion-state, sealed mid-journey rather than at any named destination

אֲשֶׁר / "where" / "wherever" (four indefinite-place constructions inside the vow)
- Role: structural indefinite-places named by Ruth across the binding ladder
- Type: indefinite-place withholding form
- Meaning: where you go / where you lodge / where you die / there I will be buried — Ruth binds herself to Naomi's path without naming any proper place
- Effect on scene: the four indefinite places hold the binding open; no PL-code is assigned

**3C — Objects and Elements**
אַל־תִּפְגְּעִי־בִי לְעָזְבֵךְ / "do not press me to leave you" — refusal-with-counter-binding opening
- What it is: prohibitive request opening Ruth's speech; refuses Naomi's directive at v.15
- Function in scene: marks Ruth's speech as refusal-of-the-release before the bindings begin
- Signals: counter-directive that refuses without giving reasons; the reasons follow as the binding ladder

לָשׁוּב מֵאַחֲרָיִךְ / "to turn back from following you"
- What it is: the action Ruth refuses to perform
- Function in scene: names what Naomi has just directed Ruth to do (turn back) and refuses it
- Signals: lexical link to Orpah's "going back" (שׁוּבִי) at v.15; refusal of the same act

אֶל־אֲשֶׁר תֵּלְכִי אֵלֵךְ / "where you go I will go" — paired path-vow with indefinite-place + matched-action form
- What it is: first binding in the six-step ladder; path-binding
- Function in scene: opens the matched indefinite-place + matched-action parallel structure
- Signals: the parallel structure is a strong rhetorical and oral feature
- Cross-ref: [[FIG_0072-Paired-Path-Lodging-Vow]]

וּבַאֲשֶׁר תָּלִינִי אָלִין / "where you lodge I will lodge"
- What it is: second binding in the ladder; lodging-binding
- Function in scene: completes the paired path-and-lodging pair under FIG_0072
- Signals: same matched indefinite-place + matched-action structure as the path-binding
- Cross-ref: [[FIG_0072-Paired-Path-Lodging-Vow]] (closes the pair within v.16)

עַמֵּךְ עַמִּי / "your people my people"
- What it is: third binding; people-binding; terse nominal equation with no copula
- Function in scene: binds Ruth to Naomi's people-collective ([[B31-People-of-YHWH]])
- Signals: first half of the people-and-God nominal-equation pair
- Cross-ref: [[CB_0021-People-and-God-Pairing]]

וֵאלֹהַיִךְ אֱלֹהָי / "your God my God"
- What it is: fourth binding; God-binding; terse nominal equation with no copula
- Function in scene: binds Ruth to Naomi's God without yet naming YHWH
- Signals: second half of the people-and-God pair; the named YHWH follows at v.17
- Cross-ref: [[CB_0021-People-and-God-Pairing]] (closes the pair within v.16)

בַּאֲשֶׁר תָּמוּתִי אָמוּת / "where you die I will die"
- What it is: fifth binding; death-place-binding
- Function in scene: extends the binding past life-orbit into mortality
- Signals: first half of the death-and-burial pair
- Cross-ref: [[FIG_0074-Death-and-Burial-Shared-Place]]

וְשָׁם אֶקָּבֵר / "and there I will be buried"
- What it is: sixth binding; burial-place-binding
- Function in scene: completes the death-and-burial pair; binding extended past death itself
- Signals: second half of the death-and-burial pair; closes the six-step ladder
- Cross-ref: [[FIG_0074-Death-and-Burial-Shared-Place]] (closes the pair within v.17a)

כֹּה יַעֲשֶׂה יְהוָה לִי וְכֹה יוֹסִיף / "may YHWH do thus to me and worse" — self-curse oath formula
- What it is: canonical Hebrew self-curse oath formula invoking the divine name
- Function in scene: seals the six-binding ladder with oath-force; first naming of YHWH on Ruth's lips
- Signals: do not soften; high terminological-consistency requirement; will recur at 3:13 (Boaz's oath)
- Cross-ref: [[CB_0020-Oath-Formula-Self-curse]], [[FIG_0075-Self-Curse-Oath-Formula]]

כִּי הַמָּוֶת יַפְרִיד בֵּינִי וּבֵינֵךְ / "if death itself separates between me and you" — oath-conditional
- What it is: the conditional clause completing the self-curse formula
- Function in scene: states the only condition under which the binding could fail
- Signals: structurally unusual — the condition is mortality itself, which is presumed inevitable; the oath therefore binds Ruth past her own death since the condition can only fail through death's separation

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B9-Ruth]] Ruth answers [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi with one continuous speech. She first refuses Naomi's directive to turn back. She then binds herself to Naomi in six successive vows: wherever Naomi walks, Ruth will walk; wherever Naomi lodges, Ruth will lodge; Naomi's people will be Ruth's people; Naomi's God will be Ruth's God; wherever Naomi dies, Ruth will die; where Naomi is buried, Ruth will be buried. She closes with the canonical self-curse oath formula invoking [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH by name: may YHWH do thus to her and worse if death itself separates her from Naomi.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Establishes Ruth's covenant-binding to Naomi as her own free verbal act, escalating from movement (path) through life-orbit (lodging), through covenantal identity (people, God), through mortality (death-place, burial-place), sealed by self-curse invoking YHWH by name.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator does not label Ruth with the Moabite epithet anywhere in this scene. The withholding at the moment of Ruth's covenantal binding does not undercut the binding by re-asserting the ethnic boundary. Ruth does not name Bethlehem or any other proper place inside the vow — all four place-bindings use indefinite-place constructions. Ruth says "your God" at v.16 without naming YHWH; the divine name appears only inside the self-curse formula at v.17. Ruth gives no reasons for her binding outside the binding itself.

### Scene 3 — Naomi's silence (v.18)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: seeing-subject who falls silent
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: observed in her resolve; the silent referent of Naomi's seeing
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Presence: PRESENT

**3B — Places**
בַּדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continued)
- Role: setting of the silence
- Type: journey locus
- Meaning: the road continues from Scenes 1 and 2
- Effect on scene: leaves the two together in motion-state at the moment Naomi stops speaking

**3C — Objects and Elements**
מִתְאַמֶּצֶת / "she was determined" — narrator interior-access verb for Ruth's resolve
- What it is: hithpael participle marking Ruth's resolved state as the narrator sees it
- Function in scene: gives the audience interior access to Ruth's resolve through Naomi's seeing
- Signals: rare narrator-interior moment; the narrator does not give interior access to Naomi at the same moment

וַתֵּרֶא / "and she saw"
- What it is: narrator's report of Naomi seeing Ruth's resolve
- Function in scene: registers Naomi's recognition without reporting her interior state
- Signals: paired with the cessation-of-speech that follows; first verb of v.18

וַתֶּחְדַּל לְדַבֵּר אֵלֶיהָ / "and she stopped speaking to her" — cessation-of-speech narrator report
- What it is: narrator's report of Naomi ceasing her appeal
- Function in scene: closes the road dialogue; second verb of v.18
- Signals: structural counterpart to the two scenes of speech that preceded; the appeals are over

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi sees that [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth is determined to go with her, and she stops speaking to her.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Marks the failure of Naomi's release-attempt and the moment after which the two continue together. Closes the road dialogue.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator reports Naomi seeing and Naomi stopping speech, but does not report Naomi's interior state. The narrator does not report agreement, does not report blessing, does not report continuing the journey, does not report acceptance, does not report any further word from Naomi inside the pericope. Naomi's silence after Ruth's vow is interpretively open and the pericope closes there.
