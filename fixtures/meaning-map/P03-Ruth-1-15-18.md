# P03 — Ruth 1:15–18

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** Naomi's last appeal; Ruth's vow; Naomi's silence
- **BCV:** Ruth 1:15–18
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
Pericope-level default is INFORMAL_CASUAL, the unmarked default register for biblical narrator voice. Scenes 1 and 2 carry scene-level INTIMATE — mother-in-law and daughter-in-law speaking face-to-face on the road, no community audience and no formal covenant venue. INTIMATE fits the social setting better than CEREMONIAL even for Ruth's vow at v.16-17, despite the institutional weight of the oath at v.17b; the binding-force of the oath is carried by the figure flags and by downstream STA encoding, not by elevated register. Scene 3 returns to the INFORMAL_CASUAL narrator default for the narrator's report of Naomi seeing and stopping speech. CONSULTATIVE is not used for narrator voice (hard rule).

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The pericope moves from Naomi's release-attempt through Ruth's covenant-binding to Naomi's silence. Naomi names Orpah's return-trajectory as the example Ruth should follow; Ruth answers with a six-step binding ladder closed by a self-curse oath; Naomi sees Ruth's resolve and stops speaking. The burden is the shift from severance to indissoluble binding, sealed inside one extended speech. The concern is whether Ruth's verbal binding to Naomi — to her people, to her God, to her death-place — will hold across what the rest of the book brings.

### 2.2 Context
The pericope picks up immediately after Orpah's exit at 1:14 (P02 P15). Same road from Moab toward the land of Judah continues. Naomi has just released the daughters-in-law twice in P02 and watched Orpah accept the release. This pericope is her last attempt and Ruth's structural answer. The four book-spanning discourse threads opened across P01-P02 (T4 hesed-answered-with-hesed, T5 Moabite-outsider-incorporation, T6 YHWH-at-work, plus T1 Naomi-security-and-rest still active) all intersect at the vow.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
Intimate and slowed. The pace narrows from P02's nine-verse density to four-verse weight, with most of the pericope held by one extended speech. The tone rises across the binding ladder from movement to mortality without ever leaving the kinship register. The closing mood is unsettled: Naomi sees, Naomi stops speaking, but the narrator gives no resolution.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
Records Ruth's covenant-binding to Naomi as her own free verbal act, before the narrative has placed her in any institutional frame. Marks the silent dabaq-cling at 1:14 as the first half of Ruth's incorporation and the vow as the verbal second half. Establishes the first explicit Moabite-outsider self-binding to Israel's people and Israel's God, and the first time the divine name appears on Ruth's lips. Speaking the passage transmits a covenant act done outside any altar, court, or community — chosen on a road, by a foreign widow, with no witness but her mother-in-law.

[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi speaks to [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth: she points to [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah (named only by the kinship form yebimtekh) having returned to her people and her gods, and directs Ruth to go back after her.

**Significant Absence**
Naomi does not name Moab as a place; she names only "her people" and "her gods." Naomi does not name Moabite gods. The unnamed-gods at v.15 stands in structural contrast with YHWH named at v.17.

### Scene 2 — Ruth's vow (v.16–17)

**3A — Beings**
[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: speaking subject; binds herself to Naomi in extended speech
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; speaks now as her own voice for the first extended speech in the book
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: addressee throughout Ruth's vow
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth; the one Ruth refuses to leave
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B10-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: invoked divine agent in the self-curse oath formula
- Relationship: named by Ruth at v.17 as the enforcer of the oath; first time YHWH appears on Ruth's lips
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B31-People-of-YHWH]] — עַמֵּךְ / "your people" (Israelite covenant people)
- Role: collective Ruth binds herself to in the people-binding at v.16
- Relationship: referenced as Naomi's people, now bound to Ruth by speech
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
בַּדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continued)
- Role: setting of Ruth's vow
- Type: journey locus
- Meaning: the road continues from Scene 1
- Effect on scene: keeps Ruth's binding in motion-state, sealed mid-journey rather than at any named destination

אֲשֶׁר / "where" / "wherever" (four indefinite-place constructions inside the vow)
- Role: structural indefinite-places named by Ruth across the binding ladder
- Type: indefinite-place withholding form
- Meaning: where you go / where you lodge / where you die / there I will be buried — Ruth binds herself to Naomi's path without naming any proper place
- Effect on scene: the four indefinite places hold the binding open; no PL-code is assigned

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene. Relocated per the content discipline (SC-0013):
  - the refusal that opens the vow → Proposition 2;
  - the "wherever you…" indefinite-place form holding four bindings → Proposition 3;
  - the six bindings (path, lodging, people, god, death-place, burial-place) → Proposition 3;
  - the people-and-god pairing → Proposition 3 ([[CB_0021-People-and-God-Pairing]], [[FIG_0072-Paired-Path-Lodging-Vow]], [[FIG_0074-Death-and-Burial-Shared-Place]]);
  - the self-curse oath and its death-only conditional → Proposition 4 ([[CB_0020-Oath-Formula-Self-curse]], [[FIG_0075-Self-Curse-Oath-Formula]]);
  - the return-road locus → continuing.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B9-Ruth]] Ruth answers [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi with one continuous speech. She first refuses Naomi's directive to turn back. She then binds herself to Naomi in six successive vows: wherever Naomi walks, Ruth will walk; wherever Naomi lodges, Ruth will lodge; Naomi's people will be Ruth's people; Naomi's God will be Ruth's God; wherever Naomi dies, Ruth will die; where Naomi is buried, Ruth will be buried. She closes with the canonical self-curse oath formula invoking [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH by name: may YHWH do thus to her and worse if death itself separates her from Naomi.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Establishes Ruth's covenant-binding to Naomi as her own free verbal act, escalating from movement (path) through life-orbit (lodging), through covenantal identity (people, God), through mortality (death-place, burial-place), sealed by self-curse invoking YHWH by name.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator does not label Ruth with the Moabite epithet anywhere in this scene. The withholding at the moment of Ruth's covenantal binding does not undercut the binding by re-asserting the ethnic boundary. Ruth does not name Bethlehem or any other proper place inside the vow — all four place-bindings use indefinite-place constructions. Ruth says "your God" at v.16 without naming YHWH; the divine name appears only inside the self-curse formula at v.17. Ruth gives no reasons for her binding outside the binding itself.

### Scene 3 — Naomi's silence (v.18)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: seeing-subject who falls silent
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: observed in her resolve; the silent referent of Naomi's seeing
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Presence: PRESENT

**3B — Places**
בַּדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continued)
- Role: setting of the silence
- Type: journey locus
- Meaning: the road continues from Scenes 1 and 2
- Effect on scene: leaves the two together in motion-state at the moment Naomi stops speaking

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene. Relocated per the content discipline (SC-0013):
  - Ruth's determined resolve → Proposition 5;
  - Naomi's ceasing to speak → Proposition 5;
  - the return-road locus → continuing from Scenes 1 and 2.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi sees that [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth is determined to go with her, and she stops speaking to her.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Marks the failure of Naomi's release-attempt and the moment after which the two continue together. Closes the road dialogue.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator reports Naomi seeing and Naomi stopping speech, but does not report Naomi's interior state. The narrator does not report agreement, does not report blessing, does not report continuing the journey, does not report acceptance, does not report any further word from Naomi inside the pericope. Naomi's silence after Ruth's vow is interpretively open and the pericope closes there.

### Proposition 3 — Ruth 1:16b–17a [Scene 2]
- **Q:** What happened? **A:** binding by vow
- **Q:** Who bound herself? **A:** [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- **Q:** To whom did she bind herself? **A:** [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- **Q:** First binding? **A:** where you go I will go (path)
- **Q:** Second binding? **A:** where you lodge I will lodge (lodging)
- **Q:** Third binding? **A:** your people will be my people ([[B31-People-of-YHWH]])
- **Q:** Fourth binding? **A:** your God will be my God (unnamed at this point in the vow)
- **Q:** Fifth binding? **A:** where you die I will die (death-place)
- **Q:** Sixth binding? **A:** and there I will be buried (burial-place)

### Proposition 4 — Ruth 1:17b [Scene 2]
- **Q:** What happened? **A:** self-curse oath-sealing
- **Q:** Who spoke the oath? **A:** [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- **Q:** Whom did Ruth invoke? **A:** [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH (first naming of YHWH on Ruth's lips)
- **Q:** Oath formula? **A:** may YHWH do thus to me and worse
- **Q:** Conditional? **A:** if death itself separates between me and you
- **Q:** Structural force? **A:** binding extends past Ruth's own death since the condition can only fail through death's separation

### Proposition 5 — Ruth 1:18 [Scene 3]
