# P04 — Ruth 1:19–22

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** Naomi and Ruth arrive in Bethlehem; the women of the town react; Naomi renames herself Mara
- **BCV:** Ruth 1:19–22
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
Pericope-level default is INFORMAL_CASUAL, the unmarked default register for biblical narrator voice. Scene 2 (Naomi's renaming lament at v.20-21) carries scene-level INTIMATE — Naomi speaks to the women of her own town from her own grief, in close-relational voice even though the setting is public; the intimate register fits the moment better than CEREMONIAL even though the renaming and the divine-name doubling carry institutional weight at the form level. Scene 1 (arrival and community recognition-question) and Scene 3 (narrator-frame close) stay at the INFORMAL_CASUAL narrator default. CONSULTATIVE is not used for narrator voice (hard rule). The institutional weight of the oath-like Shaddai/YHWH doublings and the antithetical state-word pair is carried by the figure flags in Section 5B and by downstream STA encoding, not by register.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage moves from arrival to silence. Naomi and Ruth reach Bethlehem; the whole city is stirred; the women cannot recognize Naomi; she answers their recognition-question with a public lament that refuses her own name, proposes a new bitter name, and ascribes her state twice each to YHWH and Shaddai; the narrator closes with a return-frame summary that names Ruth as the Moabitess and anchors the arrival to the beginning of the barley harvest. The burden is the gap between the public expectation of Naomi's return and Naomi's own account of who she has become. The concern is whether the harvest-frame the narrator opens at v.22 will answer the lament her voice has just spoken.

### 2.2 Context
The road dialogue is over. The journey out of Moab (begun at 1:6 in P02) reaches its destination. The town the family departed from at famine in 1:1 — and that the narrator has not named since — comes back into the story as the arrival point. The community recognition the family had at 1:2 (Ephrathite household, named by clan) fails at 1:19 (the women cannot recognize Naomi). The bread-house-in-famine irony opened at 1:1 reaches its third and final close here.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
Stilled at the arrival; then declarative under Naomi's voice; then narrator-summary at the close. The pace narrows at v.19 around the recognition-failure; rises at v.20-21 with the antithetical state-word pair and the divine-name doubling; settles back to chronicle pace at v.22. The closing mood is unresolved — Naomi's lament hangs in the air while the narrator names the harvest season opening.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
Closes chapter 1's emptying-and-return arc by bringing Naomi and Ruth into Bethlehem against the community's recognition-failure. Establishes Naomi's public account of her own bitterness, with YHWH and Shaddai both named as agents of her state. Reactivates the Moabite epithet on Ruth in narrator voice after deliberate withholding through P02 and P03. Closes the bread-house-in-famine cross-pericope figure pair. Opens the harvest-provision discourse thread at the beginning of the barley harvest, staging the work of the next pericopes.

## 3. Level 2 — Scenes / Episodes

[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: protagonist arriving at her hometown after the journey
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth; widow of [[B2-Elimelech]] Elimelech; returning from PL2 to PL1
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: accompanying Naomi; silent through the scene
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; Moabite by birth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B28-The-Whole-City]] — כָּל־הָעִיר / "all the city"
- Role: collective subject of the stirring; first appearance in the book
- Relationship: the community of Bethlehem the family departed from at 1:1
- Presence: PRESENT_COLLECTIVE

[[B24-Women-of-Bethlehem]] — (implied; the speakers of the v.19 question)
- Role: speaking subset of the stirred city; chorus-speakers asking the recognition-question; first appearance in the book
- Relationship: women of [[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]]; will recur at the blessing on Naomi at 4:14-15
- Presence: PRESENT_COLLECTIVE

**3B — Places**
[[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] — בֵּית לֶחֶם / Bethlehem
- Role: destination reached after the journey; named twice in the verse ("until they came to Bethlehem"; "when they came to Bethlehem"); first scene-anchor for Bethlehem since P01 named it as the place of departure
- Type: hometown
- Meaning: "house of bread"; the lexical bread-house name reactivates here against the famine-frame opened at 1:1
- Effect on scene: receives the returning pair; setting of the community recognition-failure

[[PL2-Fields-of-Moab]] — שְׂדֵי מוֹאָב / fields of Moab (implied)
- Role: REFERENCED implicitly as the journey-origin
- Type: foreign land
- Meaning: the country the family lived in for about ten years
- Effect on scene: framing-presence; named explicitly only at v.22 in S3

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey established in P02. The barley-harvest setting is named at v.22 in S3, not here.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth walked until they came to [[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] Bethlehem. When they arrived, [[B28-The-Whole-City]] the whole city was stirred about them. [[B24-Women-of-Bethlehem]] the women said: "Is this Naomi?"

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Stages the public arrival in Bethlehem; community recognition fails; the women's question opens the dialogue Naomi will answer in Scene 2.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator does not say why the women cannot recognize Naomi — whether grief, age, demeanor, or appearance has changed her. The narrator records no specific recognition or reaction toward Ruth at the arrival moment; she walks in beside Naomi but does not draw the community's attention to herself. No one greets either of them.

### Scene 2 — Naomi's renaming lament (v.20–21)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi (speaking; rejecting her own name)
- Role: speaker of the public lament; refuses her name and proposes a substitute
- Relationship: addressed by the women; addresses them in reply
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B24-Women-of-Bethlehem]] — (addressees throughout the speech; silent in the scene)
- Role: silent collective addressee of Naomi's reply
- Relationship: the chorus from S1, now spoken to
- Presence: PRESENT_COLLECTIVE

[[B10-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: invoked twice in Naomi's lament — as the one who brought her back empty (v.21a) and as the one who has testified against her (v.21c)
- Relationship: named by Naomi as agent of her state; same divine subject Naomi named at 1:6 (provider) and 1:13 (hand-against)
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B12-Shaddai]] — שַׁדַּי / Shaddai
- Role: invoked twice in Naomi's lament — as the one who has dealt bitterly with her (v.20) and as the one who has done evil to her (v.21d); first occurrence in the book
- Relationship: archaic-poetic divine name; same divine subject as YHWH but in different invocation-form
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
[[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] — Bethlehem (implied locus of the speech)
- Role: setting of Naomi's reply; the place she has just arrived in
- Type: hometown
- Meaning: the place Naomi "went out" from with a full household
- Effect on scene: holds the speech inside the community that knew her before

"out" / "back" — implicit movement-anchor inside Naomi's account
- Role: the trajectory Naomi names in "I went out full, brought me back empty"
- Type: implicit-place pair (from-Bethlehem / to-Bethlehem)
- Meaning: brackets the journey-arc the pericope just closed
- Effect on scene: gives Naomi's lament its structural shape

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; the speech occurs at the moment of arrival.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi said to [[B24-Women-of-Bethlehem]] the women: "Do not call me Naomi. Call me Mara, for [[B12-Shaddai]] Shaddai has dealt very bitterly with me. I went out full, and [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH brought me back empty. Why do you call me Naomi, when YHWH has testified against me and Shaddai has done evil to me?"

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Establishes Naomi's public account of her state — bitter, brought back empty, testified-against, dealt-evil-to. Refuses her given name (sweet) and proposes a substitute (bitter). Names YHWH twice and Shaddai twice as divine agents of her bitterness.

**Significant Absence**
The women do not respond to Naomi's lament. They do not adopt the new name, do not comfort her, do not bless her arrival, do not ask further. The scene closes with Naomi's words unanswered. Naomi herself gives no greeting on arrival, does not bless the women, does not introduce Ruth, does not name her husband or her sons (only "I went out full" as oblique reference). The narrator gives no assessment of Naomi's account — whether her statement that YHWH brought her back empty fits the narrator's earlier silence about divine agency, or whether her self-renaming is endorsed.

### Scene 3 — Narrator-frame close: return; Ruth-the-Moabitess; barley-harvest setting (v.22)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: subject of "Naomi returned"; named again with her own name, in the very verse after she rejected it
- Relationship: returning; mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת הַמּוֹאֲבִיָּה / Ruth the Moabitess
- Role: accompanying Naomi; named by the narrator with the Moabite epithet and the daughter-in-law kinship form
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; Moabite by birth; the epithet returns in narrator voice for the first time since 1:4
- Presence: PRESENT

**3B — Places**
[[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] — בֵּית לֶחֶם / Bethlehem
- Role: arrival point in the narrator's summary; named for the third time in the pericope
- Type: hometown
- Meaning: "house of bread"; the bread-house name lands here against the harvest-frame
- Effect on scene: closes the famine-arc opened at 1:1

[[PL2-Fields-of-Moab]] — שְׂדֵי מוֹאָב / fields of Moab
- Role: REFERENCED in the narrator's "returning from the fields of Moab" phrase
- Type: foreign land
- Meaning: the country the journey-arc closed by leaving
- Effect on scene: brackets the journey-arc as a structural unit

**3C — Objects and Elements**
[[CB_0026-Barley-Harvest-Harvest-Frame]] — קְצִיר שְׂעֹרִים / barley harvest
- What it is: the agricultural season that frames the provision to come — the harvest-frame
- Function in scene: opens the harvest-provision context at the close of the emptying-arc, against which Naomi's "empty" lament has just been spoken
- Signals: first occurrence in the book; opens the harvest thread that closes at P07; the barley-harvest setting (v.22) is the scene's time-frame in §3D

**3D — Times**
בִּתְחִלַּת קְצִיר שְׂעֹרִים / "at the beginning of the barley harvest" — agricultural-season frame
- What it is: the seasonal time-anchor for the arrival; opens the harvest-frame
- Function: anchors the rest of chapter 1 and chapters 2-3 within the barley-and-wheat harvest cycle
- Signals: working time-code (no formal TM_ pre-declared); cross-ref [[CB_0026-Barley-Harvest-Harvest-Frame]]; opens [[T7-Harvest-Provision]]

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi returned, and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth the Moabitess her daughter-in-law with her, returning from [[PL2-Fields-of-Moab]] the fields of Moab. They came to [[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] Bethlehem at the beginning of the barley harvest.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Closes the pericope with a narrator-frame summary that does three structural things at once: returns the narrator's naming over Naomi's proposed renaming; reactivates the Moabite epithet on Ruth at the public-arrival moment after the deliberate withholding at P02 and P03; opens the harvest-frame against which Naomi's lament has just been spoken.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator does not preview what the harvest will bring; does not say whether anyone in the city responds further to Naomi after her speech; does not give Ruth any voice or further action at the arrival; does not name Naomi's dead husband or sons in the return-summary, even though Naomi's speech in S2 implicitly counted them in "I went out full." Ruth has been silent through the entire pericope — first long silence after the vow at P03.
