# P04 — Ruth 1:19–22

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** Naomi and Ruth arrive in Bethlehem; the women of the town react; Naomi renames herself Mara
- **BCV:** Ruth 1:19–22
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
The whole passage sits in INFORMAL_CASUAL. Scene 2 — Naomi's renaming lament at v.20-21 — shifts to INTIMATE: she speaks to the women of her own town out of her own grief, close and personal, even though the setting is a public one; that close key fits the moment better than a ceremonial one, even though renaming herself and naming God over and over carry real weight. Scene 1 (the arrival, and the women not placing her) and Scene 3 (the narrator's closing) stay in the plain narrator key. The weight of Naomi's repeated naming of Shaddai and YHWH, and of the full-against-empty contrast, is carried by the figures in Section 5B and the deeper encoding, not by a grander way of speaking.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage moves from arrival to silence. Naomi and Ruth reach Bethlehem; the whole town is stirred; the women can hardly believe it is Naomi. When they ask, she answers with a grief-stricken speech in front of them all: she will not be called by her own name, asks to be called Mara — "bitter" — and lays her suffering, twice each, on YHWH and on Shaddai. Then the narrator closes the scene, naming Ruth the Moabite and setting the arrival right at the start of the barley harvest. What weighs on the passage is the gap between what the town expects of Naomi's homecoming and the broken woman she says she has become. The question it leaves hanging is whether the harvest the narrator names at v.22 will answer the lament she has just spoken.

### 2.2 Context
The talk on the road is over. The journey out of Moab — begun back at 1:6 — reaches its end. The town the family left in the famine at 1:1, and that the narrator hasn't named since, comes back into the story now as the place they arrive. Once the family was known here, named by clan as Ephrathites of Bethlehem (1:2); now the women can hardly tell it is Naomi (1:19). And the old irony of "the house of bread" gone hungry, opened at 1:1, comes to its third and last close right here.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The scene goes still as they arrive; then Naomi's own voice takes over, flat and hard; then the narrator steps back to close it. Things slow at v.19 when the women can't place her, lift at v.20–21 as she sets the full life she left against the empty one she's come back to and names God over and over, and settle into plain reporting at v.22. The closing mood is unresolved — Naomi's lament hangs in the air while the narrator names the harvest just beginning.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
This passage closes chapter 1's long emptying-and-return by bringing Naomi and Ruth into Bethlehem, where the town can hardly recognize Naomi. It gives Naomi's own public account of her bitterness, with both YHWH and Shaddai named as the ones who did it to her. It lets the narrator call Ruth "the Moabite" again — out loud, for the first time since 1:4, after holding the word back all through P02 and P03. It closes the running irony of the hungry "house of bread." And it opens the harvest thread at the very start of the barley harvest, setting up the work of the next few pericopes.

- Role: in the background here as the place they have come from
- Type: foreign land

- Effect on scene: felt in the background; named outright only at v.22 in S3

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the journey established in P02. The barley-harvest setting is named at v.22 in S3, not here.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth walked until they came to [[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] Bethlehem. When they arrived, [[B28-The-Whole-City]] the whole city was stirred about them. [[B24-Women-of-Bethlehem]] the women said: "Is this Naomi?"

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Shows the arrival in Bethlehem out in the open; the town can hardly recognize Naomi; the women's question opens the exchange Naomi answers in Scene 2.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator never says why the women can hardly recognize Naomi — whether grief, age, the look on her, or how worn she is has changed her. He says nothing of anyone noticing or reacting to Ruth as they come in; she walks in beside Naomi but draws no eyes to herself. No one greets either of them.

### Scene 2 — Naomi's renaming lament (v.20–21)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi (speaking; rejecting her own name)
- Role: speaker of the public lament; refuses her name and proposes a substitute
- Relationship: addressed by the women; addresses them in reply
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B24-Women-of-Bethlehem]] — (the ones Naomi speaks to; silent here)
- Role: the women Naomi answers; they say nothing in this scene
- Relationship: the same women who asked the question in S1, now spoken to
- Presence: PRESENT_COLLECTIVE

[[B10-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: named twice in Naomi's lament — the one who brought her back empty (v.21a) and the one who has testified against her (v.21c)
- Relationship: the one Naomi blames for what has happened to her; the same God she named at 1:6 (who gave bread) and 1:13 (whose hand was against her)
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B12-Shaddai]] — שַׁדַּי / Shaddai
- Role: named twice in Naomi's lament — the one who has dealt bitterly with her (v.20) and the one who has done evil to her (v.21d); first time this name is used in the book
- Relationship: an old name for God; the same God as YHWH, called by a different name
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
[[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] — Bethlehem (where the speech happens, though unnamed here)
- Role: where Naomi gives her reply; the place she has just come back to
- Type: hometown
- Meaning: the place Naomi "went out" from with a whole family
- Effect on scene: sets the speech among the people who knew her before

"out" / "back" — the going-and-coming inside Naomi's own words
- Role: the path Naomi traces in "I went out full, and he brought me back empty"
- Type: a place-pair left unnamed (out from Bethlehem / back to Bethlehem)
- Meaning: frames the whole journey the passage has just closed — out full, back empty
- Effect on scene: this out-and-back is the shape Naomi's lament is built on, the full life against the empty one

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; the speech occurs at the moment of arrival.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi said to [[B24-Women-of-Bethlehem]] the women: "Do not call me Naomi. Call me Mara, for [[B12-Shaddai]] Shaddai has dealt very bitterly with me. I went out full, and [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH brought me back empty. Why do you call me Naomi, when YHWH has testified against me and Shaddai has done evil to me?"

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Gives Naomi's own public account of how she stands — bitter, brought home empty, testified against, treated harshly. She refuses her own name, which means "sweet," and asks for a new one that means "bitter." She names YHWH twice and Shaddai twice as the ones who did this to her.

**Significant Absence**
The women say nothing back to Naomi's lament. They do not take up the new name, do not comfort her, do not welcome her home, do not ask anything more. The scene ends with her words hanging unanswered. Naomi herself gives no greeting as she arrives, does not bless the women, does not introduce Ruth, does not name her dead husband or her sons (only "I went out full" hints at them). And the narrator passes no judgment on what she says — he does not tell us whether her claim that God brought her back empty squares with his own earlier silence about God doing anything, or whether her renaming herself is right.

### Scene 3 — Narrator-frame close: return; Ruth-the-Moabitess; barley-harvest setting (v.22)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: the one who "returned"; the narrator calls her Naomi again, in the very next verse after she refused the name
- Relationship: coming home; mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת הַמּוֹאֲבִיָּה / Ruth the Moabitess
- Role: with Naomi; the narrator calls her "the Moabite" and "her daughter-in-law"
- Relationship: daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; a Moabite by birth; the narrator calls her "the Moabite" again here, for the first time since 1:4
- Presence: PRESENT

**3B — Places**
[[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] — בֵּית לֶחֶם / Bethlehem
- Role: the place they arrive in the narrator's summary; named for the third time in the passage
- Type: hometown
- Meaning: "house of bread"; the name lands here just as the harvest begins
- Effect on scene: closes the famine that opened the book at 1:1

[[PL2-Fields-of-Moab]] — שְׂדֵי מוֹאָב / fields of Moab
- Role: named in the narrator's phrase "returning from the fields of Moab"
- Type: foreign land
- Meaning: the country they have now left for good
- Effect on scene: closes off the whole journey, out to Moab and back, as one stretch

**3C — Objects and Elements**
[[CB_0026-Barley-Harvest-Harvest-Frame]] — קְצִיר שְׂעֹרִים / barley harvest
- What it is: the time of year that sets the stage for the food and provision coming next
- Function in scene: opens the harvest season just as the long emptying ends — right after Naomi has called herself "empty"
- Signals: first time in the book; opens the harvest thread that closes at P07; the barley-harvest setting (v.22) is the scene's time-frame in §3D

**3D — Times**
בִּתְחִלַּת קְצִיר שְׂעֹרִים / "at the beginning of the barley harvest" — the season of the arrival
- What it is: when the arrival happens — the opening of the barley harvest
- Function: sets the rest of chapter 1 and chapters 2-3 within the harvest season, barley first and then wheat
- Signals: a working time-label (no formal TM_ code yet); cross-ref [[CB_0026-Barley-Harvest-Harvest-Frame]]; opens [[T7-Harvest-Provision]]

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi returned, and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth the Moabitess her daughter-in-law with her, returning from [[PL2-Fields-of-Moab]] the fields of Moab. They came to [[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] Bethlehem at the beginning of the barley harvest.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Closes the passage with the narrator's own summing-up, which does three things at once: he goes on calling her Naomi, over the new name she asked for; he calls Ruth "the Moabite" again at the moment of their public arrival, after holding the word back through P02 and P03; and he opens the harvest season — the very harvest against which Naomi has just called herself empty.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator gives no hint of what the harvest will bring; does not say whether anyone in the town answers Naomi after her speech; gives Ruth no word and no further act as they arrive; and does not name Naomi's dead husband or sons in the closing summary, even though her "I went out full" in S2 was counting them. Ruth has been silent through the whole passage — her first long silence since the vow at P03.
