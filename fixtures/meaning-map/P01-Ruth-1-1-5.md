# P01 — Ruth 1:1–5

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** The famine, the family's sojourn, and the emptying of the household
- **BCV:** Ruth 1:1–5
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
The whole passage sits in INFORMAL_CASUAL, the plain default for the biblical narrator's voice — the relaxed, everyday way an old story gets told from memory. One spot shifts at v.1: the set opening phrase vayhi bi-yemei ("and it happened in the days of") lifts the voice into the formal key of an old, remembered tale — COMMUNITY_MEMORY (this marks the framing, not the register). The register itself stays INFORMAL_CASUAL all the way through; v.3 and v.5 report the deaths in a few tight words.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement
### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage opens wide, on a whole era, then narrows fast to one family's exile and its slow stripping-away. It moves step by step: famine at home, the move to a foreign land, a household of four named, the father's death, the two sons marrying foreign women, about ten years passing on foreign soil, and at last both sons dead. What weighs on the passage is the shrinking: a whole family of four narrows down, loss by loss, to one grieving woman, alone in a foreign land. The question it leaves hanging is whether anything of this family — its name, its line, its future — can outlast the one woman still standing.

### 2.2 Context
This is where the book of Ruth begins, in the days of the judges — a time the first hearers remember as disordered, with no steady leader over Israel. A famine pushes a family of Ephrathites out of Bethlehem of Judah — a town whose name means "house of bread" — and into the foreign country of Moab, a people Israel has long been at odds with. No one in the story has been introduced yet; this is the reader's way in. The questions the whole book will turn on — rescue, keeping a dying family line alive, hesed (loyal, covenant kindness), and the taking-in of an outsider — are already in the air here, though the story has not yet taken them up.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The tone is held-in and plain, the voice of someone telling it straight — and the weight builds not by saying more but by saying less. The narrator never stops to grieve, never tells us what anyone feels inside, and never says God did any of it. Each verse piles loss on loss, and the deaths are reported in words as few as the marriages were quick. By the end the mood is a quiet desolation — three deaths in five verses, the last line leaving one woman stripped of everyone at once — and whatever feeling there is, it is carried by what the line leaves unsaid.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
The passage opens the book by laying out the trouble the rest of the book has to answer: a household with its men gone, a widow with no future left, an Israelite family stuck far from home. It hands on the first great loss the story is built on, and plants the questions — the family line, the inheritance, plain survival — that drive every scene to come. To speak this passage is to set in place the people, the place, the time, and the empty space the whole story will circle. This is the groundwork: a community's memory of how the emptying began.

- Function in scene: it tells us how the family means to live in Moab

- What it is: the stretch of time this scene and the whole book take place in
- Function: it sets the opening down in a known period of Israel's past
- Signals: a disordered time, with no steady leader; the days before Israel had kings

**3E — What Happens**
A famine in the land drives [[B2-Elimelech]] Elimelech, [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi, and their two sons [[B4-Mahlon]] Mahlon and [[B5-Chilion]] Chilion from Bethlehem of Judah to sojourn in the fields of Moab.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Establishes era, family, homeland, and famine-driven exile.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator never says YHWH sent the famine or drove the family out. The book opens with no word of God doing anything.
