# E03 — Esther 2:1–4

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** The remedy proposed: a search for a new queen
- **BCV:** Esther 2:1–4
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
The whole passage sits in INFORMAL_CASUAL — the plain storyteller's voice carrying the court forward after the storm of chapter 1. There is one stretch of voiced speech: the king's young men speak their proposal aloud (v.2–4), "Let them seek out beautiful young virgins for the king…" This is courtiers advising their lord, not a decree being sealed and sent — so it stays INFORMAL_CASUAL, the everyday speech of officials floating an idea, not the FORMAL_OFFICIAL key of an edict.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement
### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage is a quiet turning of the page. The king's rage from the last scene has cooled, and in the stillness that follows he remembers Vashti — what she did, and the irreversible thing that was decreed against her. The memory leaves a gap: a throne beside the king with no one on it. Into that silence step his attendants, who propose a remedy — not comfort, not reconciliation, but a search. Let officers be sent through every province to gather the most beautiful young women to the capital, into the care of the keeper of the women, and let the one who most pleases the king reign in Vashti's place. The plan pleases the king, and he acts on it. The movement is from a loss the king broods over, to a machinery set in motion to fill it — and the whole apparatus that will eventually bring Esther to the throne is switched on here, in four verses, by men who have no idea what they are starting.

### 2.2 Context
This follows the deposing of Queen Vashti (chapter 1, pericopes E01–E02): she refused the king's summons at his feast, and on his counselors' advice was put away by an irreversible decree, so that "every man should be master in his own house." Now the king is without a queen. This pericope is the bridge between that removal and the arrival of Esther (E04): it sets up the empire-wide search and the harem at Susa, under the eunuch Hegai, into which Esther will be taken. The reader already knows the world — a Persian court that runs by law and display — and now watches that same machinery, so recently used to remove a queen, turned to finding one. No Israelite and no Jew has yet entered; God is not named here, nor anywhere in the book.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The tone opens reflective and slightly heavy — the king alone with a cooled anger and a memory — then shifts to brisk and businesslike as the courtiers speak. Their proposal has the efficient, slightly cold sound of administration: provinces, officers, gathering, a keeper, cosmetics, a selection. The young women are spoken of the way a court speaks of a problem to be solved, by their beauty and their availability, not as persons. The pace quickens through the plan and then snaps shut on the last line — "the matter pleased the king, and he did so" — a short, decisive close that turns a suggestion into imperial action with no friction at all.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
The passage moves the story from the vacancy left by Vashti to the means of filling it. It re-establishes the king as a man whose moods set the empire in motion and whose servants manage him by proposing the right thing at the right moment. It introduces the harem at Susa and Hegai its keeper — the institution and the man through whom Esther will soon pass. And it plants the selection-by-pleasing mechanism — "the girl who pleases the king shall reign" — that the next pericope will resolve in Esther's favour. To speak this passage is to set the search going: the empire reaching into every province for beautiful women, the court machinery humming, and the throne waiting to be filled.

## 3. Level 2 — Scenes / Episodes

### Scene 1 — The king remembers Vashti (v.1)

**3A — Beings**
[[B4-Ahasuerus]] — אֲחַשְׁוֵרֹושׁ / Ahasuerus
- Role: the king, alone after his anger has cooled, remembering the queen he removed
- Relationship: king of Persia and Media; the husband who decreed Vashti's removal
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B5-Vashti]] — וַשְׁתִּי / Vashti
- Role: the deposed queen, present only as the king's memory — what she did and what was decreed against her
- Relationship: the former queen of [[B4-Ahasuerus]] Ahasuerus, now put away
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
- None named in this verse. The setting is the court at Susa, carried over from the preceding scenes; no new place is introduced until the search reaches out to the provinces in Scene 2.

**3C — Objects and Elements**
The king's cooled anger — חֵמָה / heat, wrath *(scene content — the king's subsided rage, not a registry object)*
- What it is: the royal fury of chapter 1, now grown still
- Function in scene: the emotional weather that has to settle before the king can remember and the court can act
- Signals: a turning-point — the storm passing, the gap it left becoming visible

The decree against Vashti — אֲשֶׁר נִגְזַר עָלֶיהָ / what was decreed against her *(scene content — the irreversible ruling recalled, not voiced here)*
- What it is: the irrevocable judgment that removed Vashti, now remembered
- Function in scene: what the king broods on — the thing that cannot be undone, and so must be replaced
- Signals: the book's engine of irreversibility (the unchangeable Medo-Persian decree), felt here as a closed door

**3D — Times**
"After these things" — אַחַר הַדְּבָרִים הָאֵלֶּה / after these words/things *(scene content — a narrative seam, not a dated time)*
- What it is: the storyteller's bridge-phrase joining this scene to the deposing of Vashti
- Function: sets the remembering "some while after" the events of chapter 1, without a date
- Signals: a deliberate, unhurried gap — time enough for anger to cool

**3E — What Happens**
After all this, when the anger of [[B4-Ahasuerus]] King Ahasuerus had cooled, he remembered [[B5-Vashti]] Vashti — both what she had done and what had been decreed against her.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Shows the king, his rage spent, brooding over the queen he removed — opening the vacancy the rest of the passage will move to fill.

**Significant Absence**
The king remembers what Vashti *did* and what was *decreed* — but no word of regret, of missing her, or of any wish to undo it is stated. Whether he longs for her or merely registers the empty place is left blank. And God, who is nowhere named in this book, is absent here too: the king's memory turns, but nothing turns it.

### Scene 2 — The courtiers propose the search (v.2–4)

**3A — Beings**
[[B4-Ahasuerus]] — הַמֶּלֶךְ / the king
- Role: the lord to whom the plan is addressed, and who finally approves it and acts
- Relationship: king without a queen; the one the search is meant to serve
- Presence: PRESENT

The king's young men, his attendants — נַעֲרֵי הַמֶּלֶךְ מְשָׁרְתָיו / the king's servants who attended him *(unregistered group — candidate B-code; registry-delta flagged)*
- Role: the courtiers who voice the proposal — the search, the officers, the gathering, the selection
- Relationship: the personal attendants of [[B4-Ahasuerus]] the king, who manage his moods by proposing remedies
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B7-Hegai]] — הֵגַי / Hegai
- Role: the king's eunuch, keeper of the women, into whose charge the gathered virgins are to be given
- Relationship: official (saris) of [[B4-Ahasuerus]] the king; custodian of [[I4-The-house-of-the-women-the-harem]] the house of the women
- Presence: REFERENCED (named in the plan; not yet acting)

The beautiful young virgins — נְעָרֹות בְּתוּלֹות טֹובֹות מַרְאֶה / good-looking young virgins *(unregistered group — candidate B-code; registry-delta flagged)*
- Role: the object of the search — the young women to be gathered from every province for the king to choose among
- Relationship: subjects of the empire, sought by their beauty; the pool from which a new queen (and, unknown to the court, Esther) will come
- Presence: REFERENCED

The provincial officers — פְּקִידִים / officers *(unregistered group — candidate B-code; registry-delta flagged)*
- Role: the agents the king is to appoint in every province to carry out the gathering
- Relationship: appointees of [[B4-Ahasuerus]] the king, the reach of the court into the provinces
- Presence: REFERENCED (proposed, not yet appointed)

**3B — Places**
[[PL1-Susa-the-citadel]] — שׁוּשַׁן הַבִּירָה / Susa the citadel
- Role: the capital to which the gathered women are to be brought
- Type: royal capital / fortified acropolis
- Meaning: the center to which the empire's beauty is to be drawn — the throne's gravity working outward and back
- Effect on scene: makes the search converge on one place, the seat of the throne

The provinces of the kingdom — כָּל מְדִינֹות מַלְכוּתֹו / all the provinces of his kingdom *(scene content — the empire's reach; the registered institution [[I5-The-royal-provinces-the-127]] names the 127 as a whole)*
- Role: the whole extent the search is to comb for women
- Type: the administrative span of the empire
- Meaning: the same 127-province machinery used to send decrees, here sending officers to gather
- Effect on scene: stretches the search across the entire empire — nothing local about it

**3C — Objects and Elements**
[[I4-The-house-of-the-women-the-harem]] — בֵּית הַנָּשִׁים / the house of the women (the harem)
- What it is: the women's quarters at Susa where the gathered virgins are to be kept under Hegai
- Function in scene: the destination and holding-place of the search — the institution Esther will be taken into
- Signals: a managed, enclosed world of waiting women — the court's apparatus for producing a queen

The cosmetics / beauty-treatments — תַּמְרוּקֵיהֶן / their ointments, cosmetics *(scene content — the care given to the gathered women, not a registry object)*
- What it is: the ointments and beauty-preparations to be provided to the women in the harem
- Function in scene: part of the plan's machinery — the women are to be groomed for the king's choosing
- Signals: the women processed as objects of display, prepared for selection

The royal office of queen — תִּמְלֹךְ תַּחַת וַשְׁתִּי / let her reign in place of Vashti *(scene content — the vacant throne to be filled, not a registry object)*
- What it is: the queenship itself, now empty, the prize of the whole search
- Function in scene: the goal that gives the search its point — one of the gathered women to take Vashti's place
- Signals: the throne as a position to be filled by pleasing the king

**3D — Times**
- None: no separate time-setting; the proposal follows directly on the king's remembering in Scene 1. The verbs are about a plan to be carried out, not a dated moment.

**3E — What Happens**
The king's young men, his attendants, said: let beautiful young virgins be sought out for the king. Let the king appoint officers throughout all the provinces of his kingdom to gather every beautiful young virgin to [[PL1-Susa-the-citadel]] Susa the citadel, to [[I4-The-house-of-the-women-the-harem]] the house of the women, into the charge of [[B7-Hegai]] Hegai the king's eunuch, the keeper of the women — and let their cosmetics be given them; and let the young woman who pleases the king reign in place of [[B5-Vashti]] Vashti. The plan pleased the king, and he did so.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Sets the empire-wide search in motion — the officers, the gathering to the harem under Hegai, the selection-by-pleasing — and shows the king approving it, turning a suggestion into imperial action.

**Significant Absence**
No one asks whether Vashti might be restored, or whether the women have any say. The plan speaks of them entirely by beauty and availability, never by name, will, or family. And again God is not named: the search that will bring the book's deliverer to the throne is proposed and approved as pure court business, with no hint that anything but royal appetite is steering it.
