# P10 — Ruth 3:14-18

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** The nameless dawn: six measures home, and sit still
- **BCV:** Ruth 3:14-18
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

*(Versification note: Ruth's chapter and verse numbers are the same in English and Hebrew — no divergence in this pericope.)*

**Multi-level register tagging:**
The whole passage sits in INFORMAL_CASUAL. Both scenes shift to INTIMATE at scene level: the dawn at the floor is still inside the night's privacy — hushed words between the two of them before anyone can recognize anyone — and the homecoming is the two women alone again, the P07 and P08 house-talk register. There is no public moment and no ceremonial lift anywhere in the pericope. The narrator's voice frames the movements plainly.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage carries the night home and sets the story down to wait. Ruth lies at the place of his feet until morning and rises before one person could recognize another; Boaz says the night's one rule out loud — let it not be known that the woman came to the floor. Then the parting gift: hold out the cloak you are wearing; he measures six measures of barley into it and lays it on her, and he goes into the town. At home, her mother-in-law asks the strange question of the morning — who are you, my daughter? — and Ruth tells her all that the man did for her, and shows the rest: these six measures of barley he gave me, for he said, do not go empty to your mother-in-law. Naomi reads the gift and the man at once: sit still, my daughter, until you know how the matter falls — for the man will not rest unless he has finished the matter today. What weighs on the passage is how much now moves outside the women's reach: the night went unseen, the gift speaks for the man, and the matter is in his hands. The question it leaves hanging is the one Naomi tells Ruth to sit inside: how the matter will fall.

### 2.2 Context
The dawn closes the threshing-floor night under the same care that began it: no one must know, and no one in the whole pericope speaks a single name — Boaz is "the man," Ruth is "the woman" and "my daughter," Naomi is "her mother-in-law." Naomi's question — who are you, my daughter? — repeats the words Boaz asked in the dark at 3:9; the night's question comes home in the morning's mouth. The six measures answer the book's oldest wound in Boaz's own reported word: Naomi came back from Moab saying YHWH brought her back empty (reqam, 1:21), and Boaz sends Ruth home saying do not go empty (reqam) to your mother-in-law — the gift is aimed at Naomi. The narrator never shows Boaz saying those words at the floor; they reach us only through Ruth's report, the same way her 2:21 report carried more than the scene had shown. The text's own word has him go into the town first; she comes to her mother-in-law in the next breath. And Naomi's closing word — the man will not rest unless he has finished the matter today — is the bridge the next pericope crosses straight to the gate.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
Hushed, quick, and then deliberately still. The dawn moves in short motions — lying, rising before light, two low instructions, the cloak held out, the grain weighed in, the parting. The homecoming quickens with the question and the telling, the gift laid out as proof. Then the passage does something rare: it ends by slowing all the way down — sit still, my daughter — a held breath with the whole legal day in front of it. The mood is secrecy giving way to confidence: Naomi, who once called herself empty, now reads the man's intent in a measure of barley.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
This passage closes the threshing-floor scene under its rule of secrecy and hands the story to the gate. It answers the empty-word of Naomi's 1:21 lament with the same word in Boaz's reported mouth — the structural turn from emptying to filling, carried in six measures of barley. It doubles the night's "who are you?" into the morning's, letting the question carry recognition, identity, and changed standing at once — the words keep all three open. It completes the naming-down of the whole pericope: no names at all, only the man, the woman, the daughter, the mother-in-law. And it ends on the wait-formula — the man will not rest — that the gate scene immediately fulfills; to speak this passage is to keep the hush, the weighed gift, and the stillness before everything is decided.

## 3. Level 2 — Scenes / Episodes

### Scene 1 — The dawn at the floor: the secrecy word and the six measures (3:14–15)

**3A — Beings**

[[B9-Ruth]] — הָאִשָּׁה / "the woman"
- Role: the one who lies until morning, rises before light, holds out the cloak, and carries the gift to town
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; the woman of the night's petition
- Referential form: "the woman" in Boaz's secrecy word — no name spoken of her in this pericope
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B13-Boaz]] — implied speaker
- Role: the one who sets the secrecy rule, measures the six measures, and goes into the town
- Relationship: the redeemer-pledged kinsman of the night; the matter is now his to finish
- Referential form: never named in this pericope — only "he," and later "the man"
- Presence: PRESENT

**3B — Places**

[[PL6-Threshing-Floor]] — הַגֹּרֶן / the threshing floor
- Role: the place the night must not be known to have included
- Type: the open working-floor
- Effect on scene: what the secrecy word protects — "that the woman came to the floor"

[[PL4-The-City]] — הָעִיר / the town
- Role: where he goes as the dawn parts them
- Type: the town the two women live in (Bethlehem, named here only as "the town")
- Effect on scene: the gift's destination and the day's — the matter moves toward the town and its gate

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[CB_0042-Uncover-Feet-Margelot]] — מַרְגְּלֹתָיו / "the place of his feet"
- What it is: the place where the sleeper's feet lie
- Function in scene: where Ruth lay until morning — the night's last word on the position, fourth and final occurrence of the night scenes' word (3:4, 7, 8, 14)

[[O13-Your-Garments]] — הַמִּטְפַּחַת / "the cloak"
- What it is: the outer wrap Ruth is wearing — the word here is mitpachat, not the simlah of 3:3; the registry carries her outer clothing as one entity
- Function in scene: held out and filled — the garment of the preparation becomes the sack for the gift

[[O16-Six-Barley]] — שֵׁשׁ־שְׂעֹרִים / "six of barley"
- What it is: six measures of barley — the measure-unit is left unsaid by the text
- Function in scene: the parting gift, weighed into the cloak and laid on her

**3D — Times**

[[TM_NIGHT_TO_MORNING-Night-to-Morning]] — עַד־הַבֹּקֶר … בְּטֶרֶם / "until the morning … before"
- What it is: the morning of the night, caught before light
- Function: the scene's frame — the rising falls in the gray moment before one person could recognize another

**3E — What Happens**
[[B9-Ruth]] She lies at the place of his feet until the morning, and rises before one person could recognize another. [[B13-Boaz]] He says: let it not be known that the woman came to the [[PL6-Threshing-Floor]] floor. He says: hold out the cloak that is on you — and she holds it, and he measures six measures of barley and lays it on her. And he goes into the [[PL4-The-City]] town.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Closes the night under its rule — unseen, unnamed — and sends its meaning home as a weighed gift.

**Significant Absence**
No farewell is recorded, and no word about what the gift means is spoken at the floor — the do-not-go-empty reason surfaces only later, in Ruth's report. The measure-unit of the six measures is never named; the text leaves "six of barley" standing open. And no one is named: he is "he," she is "the woman."

### Scene 2 — Home: the question, the report, and "sit still" (3:16–18)

**3A — Beings**

[[B3-Naomi]] — חֲמוֹתָהּ / "her mother-in-law"
- Role: the one who asks the morning's strange question, hears the report, and reads the man in the gift
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth; the one the gift is aimed at by name of her role
- Referential form: "her mother-in-law" — her name is not spoken in this pericope
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — בִּתִּי / "my daughter"
- Role: the one questioned, the reporter of all the man did, the bearer of the six measures
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Referential form: "my daughter" in Naomi's mouth — her name is not spoken either
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B13-Boaz]] — הָאִישׁ / "the man"
- Role: the one reported on — what he did, what he gave, what he said, and what he will not rest from
- Relationship: the redeemer-pledged kinsman; the matter is his today
- Referential form: only "the man" — twice (3:16, 3:18); his name rests until the gate
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**

[[PL_NAOMIS_DWELLING-Naomis-Dwelling-in-Bethlehem]] — implied
- Role: the home the report and the counsel happen in
- Type: a private household space, not named in the text
- Effect on scene: the same closed door as the plan of 3:1–5 — the night is debriefed where it was designed

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[O16-Six-Barley]] — שֵׁשׁ־הַשְּׂעֹרִים הָאֵלֶּה / "these six of barley"
- What it is: the same six measures, now shown
- Function in scene: the gift as evidence — what Ruth lays before her mother-in-law with the man's reported word

[[CB_0044-Riqam-Empty]] — רֵיקָם / "empty"
- What it is: the empty-word — empty-handed, emptied out
- Function in scene: the word Boaz's reported speech forbids — do not go empty to your mother-in-law
- Cross-ref: Naomi's own word at 1:21, "YHWH brought me back empty"; the lament's word comes back negated, and aimed at her

[[CB_0024-Full-and-Empty]] — מְלֵאָה / רֵיקָם (the pair behind the word)
- What it is: the paired state-words — full and empty — that structured Naomi's lament
- Function in scene: the empty half is refused here; the filling has begun in barley

[[CB_0043-Mother-in-Law-Chamot]] — חֲמוֹתֵךְ / "your mother-in-law"
- What it is: the mother-in-law word — the kinship role itself
- Function in scene: the gift's stated address: Boaz's reported word names not Ruth's need but Naomi's role — the six measures are for the house

[[O19-Word]] — דָּבָר / "the matter"
- What it is: the matter — the thing now underway
- Function in scene: what Ruth must wait to see fall, and what the man will not rest until he has finished — twice named in Naomi's closing word

**3D — Times**

[[TM_TODAY-Today]] — הַיּוֹם / "today"
- What it is: the day now beginning
- Function: a content-time inside Naomi's word — the man will finish the matter today; the scene itself runs on in the same morning

**3E — What Happens**
[[B9-Ruth]] She comes to [[B3-Naomi]] her mother-in-law. Naomi says: who are you, my daughter? And Ruth tells her all that [[B13-Boaz]] the man did for her. She says: these six measures of barley he gave me, for he said to me, do not go empty to your mother-in-law. And Naomi says: sit still, my daughter, until you know how the matter falls — for the man will not rest unless he has finished the matter today.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Brings the night's meaning home — the question, the full report, the gift with its aimed word — and sets the story down to wait on the man and the day.

**Significant Absence**
Naomi's question gets no direct answer — no name, no "I am Ruth"; the report stands where the answer would be, and the words keep open whether she asks who it is at the door, or what the night has made of her. Boaz's "do not go empty" is heard only through Ruth — the narrator never showed him saying it at the floor. And the whole exchange passes without one personal name: the man, the woman, my daughter, your mother-in-law.
