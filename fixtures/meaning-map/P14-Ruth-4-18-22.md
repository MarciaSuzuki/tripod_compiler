# P14 — Ruth 4:18-22

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** The generations of Perez: ten names to David
- **BCV:** Ruth 4:18-22
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** GENEALOGY
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

*(Versification note: Ruth's chapter and verse numbers are the same in English and Hebrew — no divergence in this pericope.)*

**Multi-level register tagging:**
Pericope-level INFORMAL_CASUAL — the narrator's own chronicle voice, reciting the line in the plain "X begot Y" formula. This is the book's first GENEALOGY pericope (the genre is in the closed list; the genre_group is NARRATIVE, the narrator's frame); the weight of a king's lineage is carried by the names and the closing figures, not by any lift in the way the list is said. There are no character speeches, no ceremony, and no register shift anywhere in the unit.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage is the book's last word and its widest: a ten-name line of descent. These are the generations of Perez. Perez fathered Hezron, Hezron fathered Ram, Ram fathered Amminadab, Amminadab fathered Nahshon, Nahshon fathered Salmon, Salmon fathered Boaz, Boaz fathered Obed, Obed fathered Jesse, and Jesse fathered David. The list begins where the elders' blessing pointed — Perez, son of Judah and Tamar — and ends on the name the whole book has been quietly traveling toward: David. What weighs on the passage is its claim, made entirely by arrangement: the small Bethlehem household of a widow and a foreign daughter-in-law stands in the direct line of the king. The story's redeemed child, Obed, is the seventh name and the hinge — Boaz before him, Jesse and David after. The question the book answers here, without a sentence of comment, is the one it has carried under everything: where this rescue was going.

### 2.2 Context
This closes the book by setting its events into the long line. The genealogy is formally the toledot of Perez, the son Tamar bore to Judah — the very house the elders invoked over Boaz at the gate (4:12); the list completes the comparison they began. It repeats the Obed–Jesse–David names the neighbor-women spoke at the cradle (4:17), now seated inside a ten-generation frame that reaches back to the patriarchs. The single divine act of the book — YHWH's gift of conception (4:13) — has its long consequence spelled out here: a line to the king. The genealogy answers the prologue's distance ("in the days when the judges judged") with another reach across time, the narrator speaking from a vantage that already knows David's name.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
Even, formal, and accumulating. The repetition is the point: ten names in one unbroken cadence, each link the same shape, the list gathering weight as it goes. There is no drama in the telling and no lingering — the pace is steady and forward, name after name. The whole passage tilts toward its last word; the cadence that could run on simply stops at David, and the stop is the emphasis.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
This passage makes the book's largest claim by pure arrangement: it places Boaz, Obed, and the redeemed Bethlehem line in the ancestry of David. It completes three things the previous scenes opened — the Tamar–Perez comparison from the gate, the Obed–Jesse–David naming from the cradle, and the long consequence of YHWH's gift — and it closes the book in an inclusio with the prologue, distance answering distance. To speak this passage is to keep the plain formulaic cadence, the unbroken chain, and above all the placement of the last name: the list exists to arrive at David.

## 3. Level 2 — Scenes / Episodes

### Scene 1 — The generations of Perez (4:18–22)

**3A — Beings**

[[B27-Genealogy-Figures]] — פֶּרֶץ חֶצְרוֹן רָם עַמִּינָדָב נַחְשׁוֹן שַׂלְמָה שַׂלְמוֹן / Perez, Hezron, Ram, Amminadab, Nahshon, Salmon
- Role: the six ancestors of the line named only here, each fathering the next
- Relationship: the descent from Perez (son of Judah and Tamar) down to Salmon, Boaz's father
- Referential form: the name of Boaz's father is spelled two ways in the list — Salmah (שַׂלְמָה, 4:20) and Salmon (שַׂלְמוֹן, 4:21) — one ancestor, both forms in the text
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B13-Boaz]] — בֹּעַז / Boaz
- Role: the seventh name — the redeemer of the story, now a link in the line he served
- Relationship: son of Salmon; father of Obed
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B25-Obed]] — עוֹבֵד / Obed
- Role: the redeemed child, the line's hinge — Boaz before, Jesse and David after
- Relationship: son of Boaz and Ruth; father of Jesse
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B26-Jesse-and-David]] — יִשַׁי דָּוִד / Jesse, David
- Role: the last two names — the line's arrival; David the book's destination
- Relationship: Jesse son of Obed; David son of Jesse, the king
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
- None: no place is named in the list; the genealogy carries names, not locations.

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[CB_0049-Perez-as-Lineage-Founder]] — תּוֹלְדוֹת פָּרֶץ / "the generations of Perez"
- What it is: Perez as the head of the line — the founder of the Judah-descent the list traces
- Function in scene: the genealogy's named starting point; the house the elders invoked, now traced in full

[[CB_0005-Name-Preservation]] — the line of names
- What it is: a name kept alive by descent — the names carried forward, generation to generation
- Function in scene: the whole list is name-preservation enacted — the dead line, feared cut off, runs unbroken to the king

[[CB_0047-Obed-Name]] — עוֹבֵד / "Obed"
- What it is: the redeemed child's name, set inside the line
- Function in scene: the seventh name and the hinge — the story's child seated in the ancestry of David

[[CB_0048-David-Name]] — דָּוִד / "David"
- What it is: the line's terminal name — the king
- Function in scene: the last word of the list and the book; the destination the whole genealogy arrives at

**3D — Times**
- None: the list stands outside the story's time; the narrator recites it from a vantage that already knows the last name.

**3E — What Happens**
These are the generations of [[B27-Genealogy-Figures]] Perez. Perez fathered Hezron, and Hezron fathered Ram, and Ram fathered Amminadab, and Amminadab fathered Nahshon, and Nahshon fathered Salmon, and Salmon fathered [[B13-Boaz]] Boaz, and Boaz fathered [[B25-Obed]] Obed, and Obed fathered [[B26-Jesse-and-David]] Jesse, and Jesse fathered David.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Closes the book with a ten-name line from Perez to David, placing the redeemed Bethlehem household in the direct ancestry of the king.

**Significant Absence**
The genealogy names no mother — not Tamar, not Ruth, not Naomi — though the book it closes is theirs; the formal line carries only the fathers. And it adds no comment on David: the last name simply stops the list, the weight left for the audience to carry.
