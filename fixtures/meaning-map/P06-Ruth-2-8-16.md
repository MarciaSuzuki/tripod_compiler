# P06 — Ruth 2:8-16

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** Boaz speaks to Ruth in the field: instructions, blessing, meal, and harvester protocol
- **BCV:** Ruth 2:8-16
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
Pericope-level default is INFORMAL_CASUAL, the unmarked default for biblical narrator voice. Scene 2 (vv. 10-13) takes a scene-level INTIMATE override: Ruth's bowed-down question, Boaz's recital of her loyalty, and Ruth's response form a relational-close exchange between a foreigner and the field-owner who has just recognized her. One moment-level CEREMONIAL override at v.12: Boaz's formal blessing form invokes YHWH the God of Israel and uses the wing-of-refuge idiom. CONSULTATIVE is not used for narrator voice; the narrator's frame remains the casual chronicle default.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage moves through four steps: Boaz instructs Ruth to stay in his field under his protective regime; Ruth asks why she has found favor as a foreigner; Boaz recites what he has heard of her loyalty and blesses her under YHWH's wings; the two share a meal of abundance; Boaz then instructs his harvesters to enable her gleaning. The shape is a paired-instruction frame around a recognition-and-blessing center. The burden is the gap between Ruth's status (foreigner, gleaner) and the welcome she receives (favor, food, protection, blessing). The concern is whether the protective regime and the blessing point toward something more than provision for one day.

### 2.2 Context
The pericope follows directly on Boaz's arrival in his field and the foreman's report identifying Ruth as the Moabite woman who returned with Naomi. Naomi's household status is empty; Ruth has taken the gleaning initiative; the audience knows Boaz is a clansman of the dead husband, though Ruth does not. The Moabite identity that the foreman has named is the structural condition under which Boaz now speaks. The wing-of-refuge blessing at v.12 opens a cross-pericope pair that will close at 3:9 when Ruth asks Boaz to spread his wing over her.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The tone is warm and restrained, weighted by what Ruth's foreignness puts at stake. Boaz speaks in protective directives; Ruth speaks from the ground. The meal at v.14 brings a moment of slowed concrete welcome — sit, eat, dip, be handed grain. The closing instruction to the harvesters returns to the protective register. Pace is slow through the speeches and steady through the meal and the closing protocol.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
The pericope advances the harvest-provision thread by major degree: Boaz extends gleaning beyond legal minimum. It advances the outsider-incorporation thread by recognizing Ruth's loyalty in Boaz's own voice. It opens the wing-of-refuge cross-pericope pair that will land at 3:9. It opens the shifchah-amah status-shift pair that will also land at 3:9. It distributes the three-prohibition cluster (touch / shame / rebuke) across the passage. It plants signs of welcome toward Naomi's household without yet naming a redemption frame to the characters.

**Significant Absence**

Ruth falls on her face and asks why she has found favor and been recognized when she is a foreigner. He blesses her: may YHWH repay her work; may her wages be full from YHWH the God of Israel, under whose wings she has come for refuge. Ruth answers that she has found favor, that he has comforted her and spoken to her heart, though she is not as one of his servant-girls.

**Significant Absence**
