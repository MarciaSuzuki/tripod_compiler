# P02 — Ruth 1:6–14

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** The return road begins; Naomi urges her daughters-in-law back; Orpah turns, Ruth clings
- **BCV:** Ruth 1:6–14
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
Pericope-level default is INFORMAL_CASUAL — the unmarked default for biblical narrator voice. Scene 1 (the news and the departure) stays at the default narrator register. Scenes 2 and 3 take a scene-level INTIMATE override: the action is mother-and-daughters-in-law on the road, with kiss, weeping, and direct address ("my daughters"). The blessings at 1:8-9 carry institutional weight as blessings but are spoken inside this intimate setting, not in a ceremonial venue; the INTIMATE override fits the moment better than CEREMONIAL. Naomi's lament-framed argument at 1:11-13 stays within the same intimate register; the lament content is held by the speech-act and the hand-of-YHWH ascription rather than by a separate register shift.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage moves from a single piece of news into a journey, and from the journey into a road-side argument in two waves. Naomi hears that YHWH has given bread to his people in Judah; she rises and sets out with both daughters-in-law to return. On the road she stops twice to urge them back. The first appeal is a double blessing in YHWH's name — hesed and rest. The second is an argued dismissal grounded in the womb she does not have, the husband she cannot take, and the hand of YHWH against her. The arc traces a separation that the daughters resist; the company splits when Orpah kisses Naomi and turns back while Ruth clings to her.

### 2.2 Context
This continues directly from P01. The household is reduced to three widows in Moab; Naomi is Ephrathite from Bethlehem of Judah; Orpah and Ruth are Moabite. The famine that drove the family to Moab has ended in the land of Judah: YHWH has visited his people with bread. The whole-book questions of security, line of inheritance, outsider incorporation, and divine action — opened or threatened in P01 — now become active in Naomi's speech. The narrative locus is a road from Moab toward Judah; the narrator does not name a season or a day.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The tone shifts twice. Verses 6-7 are brisk and chronicle-like — rising, hearing, going out, walking, in tight succession. From 1:8 the pace slows and the register becomes intimate; both appeals are emotionally heavy, with vocal weeping at both. Naomi's voice moves from blessing to argued dissuasion to lament under divine agency. The pericope closes on a single line that contrasts two parallel acts on one verse — a kiss of farewell and a clinging.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
Names YHWH's first explicit action in the book and the news that triggers Naomi's return road. Opens the question of whether YHWH is at work in this story, the question of hesed answered by hesed, and the question of rest in a husband's house — each at the verse where Naomi names it. Closes the irony of the bread-house in famine opened at 1:1: the place named for plenty has received bread. Establishes the choice that defines Ruth across the rest of the book: the cling that distinguishes her from Orpah's exit. Plants Naomi's lament-framing of her situation under the hand of YHWH, which will recur and intensify at 1:20-21.

### Scene 2 — First appeal and refusal (v.8–10)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: speaker; blesser; one who urges return and kisses farewell
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B8-Orpah]] — עָרְפָּה / Orpah
- Role: addressee of the blessing and directive; weeps; refuses
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: addressee of the blessing and directive; weeps; refuses
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B10-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: invoked twice as blesser (hesed-blesser; rest-giver)
- Relationship: covenant God Naomi invokes on the daughters' behalf
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B2-Elimelech]], [[B4-Mahlon]], [[B5-Chilion]] — collectively הַמֵּתִים / "the dead"
- Role: prior recipients of the daughters-in-law's hesed (referenced in Naomi's blessing-grounding clause)
- Relationship: deceased husband of Naomi and deceased husbands implied for the daughters-in-law
- Presence: REFERENCED (named in P01; named here only as "the dead")

[[B31-People-of-YHWH]] — לְעַמֵּךְ / "your people"
- Role: recipients of the daughters' counter-declaration in v.10
- Relationship: Naomi's people; the Israelite community
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
[[PL_EACH_HER_MOTHERS_HOUSE]] — אִשָּׁה לְבֵית אִמָּהּ / "each one to her own mother's house"
- Role: distributive destination Naomi names in the first directive
- Type: distributive location (one mother's house per daughter-in-law)
- Meaning: female-relational household frame
- Effect on scene: marks the home Naomi wants the daughters to return to

הַדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continuing)
- Role: locus of the speeches
- Type: travel route
- Meaning: the road back, where the appeal happens
- Effect on scene: keeps the dialogue outside any settled household

**3C — Objects and Elements**
לֵכְנָה שֹּׁבְנָה אִשָּׁה לְבֵית אִמָּהּ / "go, return, each to her mother's house" — directive-with-distributive
- What it is: Naomi's first directive, sending each daughter-in-law to her own mother's house
- Function in scene: opens the appeal by naming the destination she proposes for them
- Signals: female-relational household frame instead of the more common "father's house"; uses [[CB_0013-Mothers-House]]

יַעַשׂ יְהוָה עִמָּכֶם חֶסֶד / "may YHWH deal hesed with you" — hesed-blessing formula
- What it is: first invocation of hesed in the book, voiced as Naomi's blessing through YHWH
- Function in scene: opens the hesed thread; couples the blessing to the prior-hesed grounding clause
- Signals: institutional blessing weight; introduces the book's central abstract noun
- Cross-ref: [[CB_0011-Hesed]]; [[CB_0008-Blessing]]

כַּאֲשֶׁר עֲשִׂיתֶם עִם־הַמֵּתִים וְעִמָּדִי / "as you have dealt with the dead and with me" — prior-hesed grounding clause
- What it is: Naomi's basis for the hesed blessing — the daughters' prior covenant-loyalty toward the dead and toward her
- Function in scene: grounds the present blessing in past practice of hesed; the structural opening of the hesed-answered-with-hesed thread
- Signals: presupposes a history of hesed not narrated; named only here

יִתֵּן יְהוָה לָכֶם וּמְצֶאןָ מְנוּחָה אִשָּׁה בֵּית אִישָׁהּ / "may YHWH grant ... rest, each in the house of her husband" — rest-blessing formula
- What it is: Naomi's second blessing — a wish for menucha (rest) for each daughter in a future husband's house
- Function in scene: opens the rest-and-husband thread; names the goal Naomi seeks for them
- Signals: distributive form again ("each in the house of her husband"); pairs structurally with the hesed blessing
- Cross-ref: [[CB_0014-Rest-Menucha]]; [[CB_0008-Blessing]]

בֵּית אִישָׁהּ / "the house of her husband" — distributive hypothetical household
- What it is: the household-of-future-husband that Naomi blesses them to find
- Function in scene: locus of the rest the blessing seeks; hypothetical per addressee, not a present place
- Signals: parallel distributive structure with mother's house; framed as the rest-locus

וַתִּשַּׁק לָהֶן / "she kissed them" — physical farewell gesture
- What it is: Naomi's kiss to her daughters-in-law
- Function in scene: physical seal of the appeal; gesture of release
- Signals: parallel with Orpah's kiss to Naomi at v.14 (reversed direction)

וַתִּשֶּׂאנָה קוֹלָן וַתִּבְכֶּינָה / "they lifted up their voices and wept" — vocal weeping formula
- What it is: the daughters' first vocal weeping
- Function in scene: collective response that prepares the refusal
- Signals: the formula will repeat at v.14, marking the parallel between the two appeals

כִּי־אִתָּךְ נָשׁוּב לְעַמֵּךְ / "no — with you we will return to your people" — refusal-with-counter-declaration
- What it is: the daughters' joint refusal of Naomi's directive, with a counter-declaration
- Function in scene: rejects "return to your mother's house" and substitutes "return with you to your people"
- Signals: the counter-declaration foregrounds Naomi's people (Israel) as the daughters' chosen destination; pre-figures Ruth's later people-binding and the eventual outsider-incorporation question

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is the speech-and-response moment on the road, continuous with Scene 1.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi tells her two daughters-in-law [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth to go and return each to her mother's house. She invokes [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH to deal hesed with them as they have dealt with the dead and with her, and to grant them rest each in the house of her husband. She kisses them. They lift up their voices and weep. They say: no — with [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi they will return to [[B31-People-of-YHWH]] her people.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Records Naomi's first appeal — a directive grounded in a double blessing — and the daughters-in-law's first vocal refusal. Opens the hesed thread and the rest-in-husband-house thread by name.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator does not name what hesed the daughters-in-law had practiced toward the dead and toward Naomi; that history is presupposed but not narrated. The narrator does not record any interior state for Orpah or Ruth at the refusal moment — only their collective "they said."

**3B — Places**
הַדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continuing)
- Role: locus of the second appeal and the split
- Type: travel route

**3C — Objects and Elements**
שֹׁבְנָה בְנֹתַי / "turn back, my daughters" — repeated address-directive
- What it is: Naomi's repeated directive (v.11, v.12) with the intimate address form "my daughters"
- Function in scene: opens and frames Naomi's expanded appeal; the address form softens the dismissal
- Signals: the repetition marks insistence; the address form sustains the intimate register

הַעוֹד־לִי בָנִים בְּמֵעַי וְהָיוּ לָכֶם לַאֲנָשִׁים / "are there yet sons in my womb who could become your husbands?" — womb-rhetorical question of dissuasion
- What it is: Naomi's rhetorical question grounded in her own womb
- Function in scene: dissuades the daughters by exposing the impossibility of a levirate-style remarriage through her
- Signals: explicit reference to the line-of-Elimelech question; negation form

זָקַנְתִּי מִהְיוֹת לְאִישׁ / "I am too old to belong to a husband" — age-impossibility statement
- What it is: Naomi's statement of her own impossibility
- Function in scene: reinforces the dissuasion with a personal fact

גַּם הָיִיתִי הַלַּיְלָה לְאִישׁ וְגַם יָלַדְתִּי בָנִים / "even if tonight I should belong to a husband and even bear sons" — hypothetical extreme-case sequence
- What it is: Naomi's hypothetical concession, granted only to be denied
- Function in scene: extends the dissuasion to the strongest imaginable case
- Signals: "tonight" (הַלַּיְלָה) is content inside the hypothetical, not a scene-setting time

הֲלָהֵן תְּשַׂבֵּרְנָה ... הֲלָהֵן תֵּעָגֵנָה / "would you wait for them ... would you shut yourselves off?" — twin rhetorical questions of dissuasion
- What it is: paired rhetorical questions following the hypothetical
- Function in scene: closes Naomi's argument with the rhetorical impossibility of the daughters' waiting
- Signals: the paired form intensifies the dissuasion

אַל בְּנֹתַי כִּי־מַר־לִי מְאֹד מִכֶּם / "no, my daughters, for it is far more bitter for me than for you" — lament-observation
- What it is: Naomi's lament-observation comparing her bitterness to theirs
- Function in scene: shifts the speech from dissuasion to lament; opens the bitter-self framing that recurs in P04

כִּי־יָצְאָה בִי יַד־יְהוָה / "because the hand of YHWH has gone out against me" — lament-frame divine ascription
- What it is: Naomi's attribution of her bitterness to YHWH's direct action
- Function in scene: closes the lament by naming a divine agent; first use of the hand-of-YHWH idiom in the book
- Cross-ref: [[CB_0015-Hand-of-YHWH]]

וַתִּשֶּׂנָה קוֹלָן וַתִּבְכֶּינָה עוֹד / "they lifted up their voices and wept again" — second vocal weeping formula
- What it is: parallel-with-v.9 weeping formula, marked by "again"
- Function in scene: closes the speech with the same vocal response as the first appeal
- Signals: the "again" makes the parallel structural

וַתִּשַּׁק עָרְפָּה לַחֲמוֹתָהּ / "Orpah kissed her mother-in-law" — closing-farewell gesture
- What it is: Orpah's kiss to Naomi (reversed direction from Naomi's kiss at v.9)
- Function in scene: marks Orpah's exit; the kiss is now the seal of departure rather than of release
- Signals: structural parallel and reversal with the kiss at v.9; uses the kinship term חֲמוֹתָהּ (her mother-in-law)

וְרוּת דָּבְקָה בָּהּ / "but Ruth clung to her" — dabaq-clinging act
- What it is: Ruth's clinging to Naomi
- Function in scene: marks Ruth's defining act; refuses the dismissal without verbal counter-declaration here
- Signals: first occurrence of the dabaq root in the book; opens the image-rhyme that will close at 2:23
- Cross-ref: [[CB_0018-Devek-Clinging]]; opens [[FIG_0012-Clinging-Dabaq-Image-rhyme]]

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the road-side moment of Scene 2.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi tells her daughters to turn back. She asks whether she has yet sons in her womb who could become their husbands. She says she is too old to belong to a husband. She grants the hypothetical that even tonight she might belong to a husband and bear sons, and asks whether they would wait for those sons or shut themselves off from having husbands meanwhile. She says it is far more bitter for her than for them, because the hand of [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH has gone out against her. They lift up their voices and weep again. [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah kisses her mother-in-law. [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth clings to her.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Records Naomi's argued second appeal — directives, rhetorical dissuasions, hypothetical sequence, and lament with divine attribution — and the split between the two daughters-in-law. Establishes Ruth's clinging as the defining act that distinguishes her from Orpah.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator does not say what Orpah felt as she turned back, only that she kissed Naomi. The narrator does not say what Ruth felt as she clung, only that she clung. The narrator does not say where Orpah went; "turning back" closes her presence in the book without a destination scene. The narrator does not directly answer Naomi's lament-attribution to YHWH; the speech ends, and the action splits.
