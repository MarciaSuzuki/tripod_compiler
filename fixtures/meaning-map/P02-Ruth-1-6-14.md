# P02 — Ruth 1:6–14

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** The return road begins; Naomi urges her daughters-in-law back; Orpah turns, Ruth clings
- **BCV:** Ruth 1:6–14
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

**Multi-level register tagging:**
The whole passage sits in INFORMAL_CASUAL, the plain default for the biblical narrator's voice. Scene 1 — the news and the setting out — stays in that plain key. Scenes 2 and 3 shift to INTIMATE: now it is a mother and her two daughters-in-law on the road, close up, with a kiss, weeping, and Naomi speaking straight to them ("my daughters"). The blessings at 1:8-9 carry real weight as blessings, but they are spoken in this close, family moment, not at a formal ceremony — so INTIMATE fits better than a ceremonial key. Naomi's hard, argued plea at 1:11-13 stays in that same close register; the grief is carried by what she says and by her laying her trouble on the hand of YHWH, not by another change of key.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage moves from a single piece of news into a journey, and from the journey into an argument on the road that comes in two waves. Naomi hears that YHWH has given bread to his people back in Judah; she gets up and starts home with both daughters-in-law. On the road she stops twice and tries to send them back. The first time she does it with a blessing in YHWH's name — two things she asks for them: hesed (loyal, covenant kindness) and rest. The second time she argues them out of it: she has no more sons in her, she is too old for a husband, and the hand of YHWH has turned against her. The whole movement is a parting the daughters fight; in the end the company breaks in two when Orpah kisses Naomi and turns for home while Ruth holds on to her and will not let go.

### 2.2 Context
This picks up right where P01 left off. The household is down to three widows in Moab: Naomi, of the old clan of Bethlehem of Judah, and her two Moabite daughters-in-law, Orpah and Ruth. The famine that drove the family to Moab is over back home — YHWH has come to the help of his people and given them bread. The questions the whole book turns on — a safe place to live, keeping a dying family line alive, an outsider being taken in, and whether God is at work in any of this — were planted in P01; here they come alive in what Naomi says. The scene is a road running from Moab back toward Judah. The narrator names no season and no day.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The tone shifts twice. Verses 6-7 are quick and plain — hearing the news, getting up, going out, walking, one thing after another. From 1:8 the pace slows down and the telling moves in close; both of Naomi's pleas are heavy with feeling, and there is loud weeping at each. Her voice changes as she goes: first she blesses the daughters, then she argues them out of following her, and at last she lays her bitterness on the hand of God. The passage ends on a single line that sets two acts side by side — Orpah's kiss goodbye, and Ruth's holding on.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
This passage names the first thing the book says God plainly does — and the news of it is what turns Naomi toward home. It opens three questions, each at the verse where Naomi first says it: whether God is at work in this story at all, whether the daughters' loyal kindness will be answered with the same, and whether they will find rest in a home of their own. It closes the irony the book opened at 1:1: Bethlehem, "the house of bread," has bread again. It sets up the choice that marks Ruth for the rest of the book — the holding-on that sets her apart from Orpah's turning back. And it plants the way Naomi reads her own suffering, as the hand of God against her — something she will say again, and harder, at 1:20-21.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi hears in [[PL2-Fields-of-Moab]] the fields of Moab that [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH has visited [[B31-People-of-YHWH]] his people in giving them bread. She rises with her two daughters-in-law [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth to return from the fields of Moab. **3F — Communicative Purpose**
Names the first explicit act of YHWH in the book and the news that triggers Naomi's return road.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator never says how Naomi heard the news, or who told her. He never tells us what she felt when she heard it — only what she did. And he does not say God brought Naomi home: what God does is give bread to his people in Judah, and Naomi gets up and goes on her own.

- What it is: hesed — loyal, covenant kindness; the faithful goodness that does more for someone than duty alone would ask
- Function in scene: what Naomi asks God to show the daughters, the same kindness they showed her and her dead
- Signals: the book's key word for loyal love; first named here at 1:8; it is the same word again at 2:20 and 3:10

[[CB_0008-Blessing]] — blessing
- What it is: calling down God's favor on someone
- Function in scene: the shape Naomi's two wishes take — kindness and rest, asked for in YHWH's name
- Signals: more than a casual good-wish; the same kind of blessing comes back at 2:12, 3:10, and 4:11–14

[[CB_0014-Rest-Menucha]] — מְנוּחָה / rest
- What it is: a settled, secure place to live — a home of one's own
- Function in scene: the second thing Naomi wishes for them, a safe home each with a husband
- Signals: the very thing Naomi will seek again for Ruth at 3:1; a key word for safety and belonging

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is the speech-and-response moment on the road, continuous with Scene 1.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi tells her two daughters-in-law [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth to go and return each to her mother's house. She invokes [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH to deal hesed with them as they have dealt with the dead and with her, and to grant them rest each in the house of her husband. She kisses them. They lift up their voices and weep. They say: no — with [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi they will return to [[B31-People-of-YHWH]] her people.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Records Naomi's first plea — a command to go home, wrapped in a double blessing — and the daughters-in-law's first out-loud refusal. Names two threads the book will follow: loyal kindness, and rest in a home of one's own.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator never tells us what kindness the daughters-in-law had actually shown the dead and Naomi; it is taken for granted but never spelled out. And he gives us nothing of what Orpah or Ruth felt as they refused — only the two of them together saying "no."

### Scene 3 — Second appeal and the split (v.11–14)

**3A — Beings**
[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי / Naomi
- Role: speaker; arguer; lamenter
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah and [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B8-Orpah]] — עָרְפָּה / Orpah
- Role: addressee; weeps; kisses Naomi; turns back (exit moment from the book)
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Presence: PRESENT → DEPARTS

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: addressee; weeps; clings (davqah) to Naomi
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B10-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: the one Naomi blames for her bitterness at 1:13
- Relationship: the God whose "hand has gone out against" her, in her own words
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**
הַדֶּרֶךְ / the road (continuing)
- Role: locus of the second appeal and the split
- Type: travel route
- Meaning: the road back; the place where the company divides
- Effect on scene: keeps the division visible as a roadside event

**3C — Objects and Elements**
- None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame for this scene; time is continuous with the road-side moment of Scene 2.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi tells her daughters to turn back. She asks whether she has yet sons in her womb who could become their husbands. She says she is too old to belong to a husband. She grants the hypothetical that even tonight she might belong to a husband and bear sons, and asks whether they would wait for those sons or shut themselves off from having husbands meanwhile. She says it is far more bitter for her than for them, because the hand of [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH has gone out against her. They lift up their voices and weep again. [[B8-Orpah]] Orpah kisses her mother-in-law. [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth clings to her.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Records Naomi's second plea — this time argued: she tells them to turn back, asks the hard questions that leave them no reason to follow, walks through the impossible hope of more sons, and ends by laying her bitterness on God — and then the parting of the two daughters-in-law. Sets Ruth's holding-on as the act that marks her off from Orpah.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator never says what Orpah felt as she turned back — only that she kissed Naomi. He never says what Ruth felt as she held on — only that she held on. He never says where Orpah went; she turns back, and that is the last we see of her, with no scene to send her home to. And he never answers Naomi's charge that God is against her; her speech simply ends, and the company splits.
