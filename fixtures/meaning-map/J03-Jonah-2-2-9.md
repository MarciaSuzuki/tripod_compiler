# J03 — Jonah 2:2–9

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** The prayer from the deep: down to Sheol, up from the pit
- **BCV:** Jonah 2:2–9
- **Genre Group:** POETIC_SUNG
- **Genre:** PRAYER

*(Versification note: English 2:2–9 is Hebrew 2:3–10 — every verse in this pericope diverges by one.
The verse references below are English; each movement heading carries the Hebrew range. One narrative
word — "and he said" — rides the front of the first verse; the poem proper begins at "I called.")*

- **Register:** RELIGIOUS_WORSHIP

**Multi-level register tagging:**
This pericope has no narrator voice: by the ruled division it is the poem alone, a prayer spoken to YHWH from start to finish, so the pericope-level register is RELIGIOUS_WORSHIP — the voice of worship addressed directly to God. There is no storytelling voice inside this pericope; the one narrative word ("and he said") is the seam left by cutting at the verse boundary, not a narrating presence. No scene-level or moment-level shifts: the prayer holds one register throughout — recounting, confessing, and vowing all inside worship.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The prayer tells its whole story in the first breath — I called, he answered — and then walks the road again slowly: hurled into the deep, the seas closing, banished from God's eyes yet turning toward his temple; down past the weeds, down to the roots of the mountains, the earth's bars shut behind — and then the lift, "you brought my life up from the pit." At the bottom the drowning man remembered YHWH, and the prayer that rose from the deep reached the holy temple. It ends looking outward and forward: those who hold to worthless idols walk away from the loyal love that was theirs; the speaker will sacrifice with a voice of thanksgiving and pay what he vowed. The last word is the book's thesis spoken from inside a fish: salvation belongs to YHWH.

### 2.2 Context
This is the prayer J02 announced: Jonah prays to YHWH from the belly of the fish, and these are the words. The drowning the poem describes is what the sea did to him after the sailors hurled him in — the fish is not the danger but the rescue, and the prayer gives thanks for a deliverance already worked. Sheol is the place of the dead, spoken of as down beneath the seas; the pit is its mouth. The holy temple is where prayer is heard — the very presence Jonah fled in J01, now the one direction he looks. Hesed is loyal, covenant kindness; "their hesed" is the loyal love idol-keepers abandon. A vow paid with a sacrifice of thanksgiving is the worshiper's public completion of a rescue — what the sailors did on the deck in J02, Jonah now promises from the deep.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The terror is all remembered — the poem speaks from the far side of it, and thanksgiving rides over the fear at every line. The waters rise through the middle movements — flood around, breakers and waves over, water to the throat, weeds at the head, down to the mountains' roots — each line one notch deeper and slower, until the bars of the earth close "forever." Then one line reverses everything: "you brought my life up from the pit." The pace turns with it: the remembering is quiet, the prayer's arrival at the temple is calm, and the close is firm and bright — a vow spoken with a steady voice, ending on the single loudest claim in the book.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
The passage is the turn of the whole story: the runaway who would not pray on deck prays from the deep, and the prayer the storm could not force out of him rises freely from the fish. It answers J01's question of whether a man can flee from before YHWH — banished from before his eyes, Jonah finds the flight ended at the bottom and the temple still in reach. It hands the book its thesis line, "salvation belongs to YHWH," before Nineveh ever hears a word. To speak this passage is to keep the shape of a thanksgiving: the rescue stated first, the distress walked through after, the bottom touched, the lift, and the vow — terror recounted inside gratitude, never the other way around.

## 3. Level 2 — Scenes / Episodes

### Scene 1 — The call answered: the cry from Sheol's belly (2:2; Heb 2:3)

**3A — Beings**
[[B1-Jonah]] — יוֹנָה / Jonah
- Role: the one praying — he called from his distress and was answered
- Relationship: the swallowed runaway of J02, now the worshiper
- Presence: PRESENT
- Referential form: the praying "I" — the poem never names him

[[B2-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: the one called to; the one who answered and heard
- Relationship: the God fled from in J01, now prayed to
- Presence: PRESENT
- Referential form: named, then addressed — the verse turns from "he answered" to "you heard"

**3B — Places**
[[PL6-Sheol]] — בֶּטֶן שְׁאוֹל / the belly of Sheol
- Role: where the cry came from — the place of the dead, spoken as the depth he cried out of
- Type: the underworld, beneath the seas
- Effect on scene: it sets the prayer's starting floor — the call rose from as far down as a voice can go

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no time-setting; the prayer recounts, it does not date.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B1-Jonah]] Jonah opens the prayer with the whole story told small: I called to [[B2-YHWH]] YHWH out of my distress, and he answered me; from the belly of [[PL6-Sheol]] Sheol I cried for help — you heard my voice. The address turns mid-verse from speaking about YHWH to speaking to him.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
States the rescue before the road: the answered call is the headline, and everything after walks back through how deep the cry came from.

**Significant Absence**
The prayer never says where the speaker is — the fish is not mentioned in the poem, here or anywhere. The distress is named, never explained: nothing of the storm, the sailors, or why he was in the sea at all.

### Scene 2 — Hurled into the deep — banished, yet turning to the temple (2:3–4; Heb 2:4–5)

**3A — Beings**
[[B1-Jonah]] — יוֹנָה / Jonah
- Role: the one cast into the deep; the banished one who turns toward the temple
- Relationship: the praying "I", recounting
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B2-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: the one who did the casting — "you cast me"; the one whose eyes banished him; the owner of the temple
- Relationship: addressed throughout — the breakers and waves are "yours"
- Presence: PRESENT

**3B — Places**
[[PL4-Sea]] — יַמִּים / the seas
- Role: where he was cast — "into the deep, into the heart of the seas"
- Type: open water; the same sea of J02, now spoken from inside
- Effect on scene: the deep is the sentence carried out — the sea the storm wanted him in has him

[[PL7-Temple]] — הֵיכַל קָדְשְׁךָ / your holy temple
- Role: where the banished man says he will look again
- Type: the place of YHWH's presence, where prayer is heard
- Effect on scene: it is the turn — driven from God's eyes, he points his face at God's house

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: no time-setting; the recounting runs straight on.

**3E — What Happens**
You cast me into the deep, into the heart of the [[PL4-Sea]] seas, and the flood was all around me; all your breakers and your waves passed over me. And I said: I am driven away from before your eyes — yet I will look again toward your holy [[PL7-Temple]] temple.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Names the thrower: what the sailors' hands did, the prayer credits to YHWH — the sea was his sentence. And it sets the prayer's hinge: banishment answered not with despair but with a face turned toward the temple.

**Significant Absence**
The sailors who hurled him are gone from the telling — "you cast me" gives YHWH their act, and no human hand is mentioned. The banishment is stated without protest: there is no "why?", no plea against the sentence, and no asking to be spared.

### Scene 3 — The bottom and the lift: down to the roots, up from the pit (2:5–7; Heb 2:6–8)

**3A — Beings**
[[B1-Jonah]] — יוֹנָה / Jonah
- Role: the drowning man — engulfed, wrapped, sunk to the roots; then lifted, remembering, heard
- Relationship: the praying "I" at his lowest point
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B2-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: the one who brought his life up from the pit; the one remembered; the one the prayer reached
- Relationship: addressed — "YHWH my God"
- Presence: PRESENT
- Referential form: "YHWH my God" — the runaway's first "my" of the book

**3B — Places**
[[PL8-Pit]] — שַׁחַת / the pit
- Role: what his life was brought up from
- Type: the grave's mouth; Sheol's door
- Effect on scene: it marks the lowest point — the lift is measured from here

[[PL7-Temple]] — הֵיכַל קָדְשְׁךָ / your holy temple
- Role: where the fainting man's prayer arrived
- Type: the place of YHWH's presence
- Effect on scene: the looked-for temple of the last scene now receives the prayer — the turn completed

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[O7-Deep]] — תְּהוֹם / the deep
- What it is: the primeval deep — the great water under everything
- Function in scene: it closed around him; the drowning at its widest

[[O8-Weeds]] — סוּף / the weeds
- What it is: the sea-wrack of the bottom
- Function in scene: wrapped around his head — the bottom touching him

**3D — Times**
- None: no time-setting; "forever" is the speaker's word for how final the bars felt, not a date.

**3E — What Happens**
The waters closed around me up to the throat; the [[O7-Deep]] deep surrounded me; [[O8-Weeds]] weeds were wrapped around my head. I went down to the roots of the mountains; the earth — her bars were behind me forever. And you brought my life up from the [[PL8-Pit]] pit, [[B2-YHWH]] YHWH my God. As my soul fainted within me, I remembered YHWH; and my prayer came to you, to your holy [[PL7-Temple]] temple.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Touches bottom so the lift can be measured: three lines down, one line up. The remembering at the faint — not at the start — is the honest center: rescue came at the end of strength, and the prayer crossed the whole distance from the roots of the mountains to the temple.

**Significant Absence**
There is no cry for help at the bottom — the poem records sinking, fainting, and remembering, but no plea; the lift is given before any asking is told. And still no word of guilt: the man dying under God's waves never says the flight was wrong.

**3A — Beings**

- Role: the one vowing — sacrifice with thanksgiving, payment of what he vowed
- Relationship: the rescued man, speaking forward now
- Presence: PRESENT
- Referential form: "but I" — set against the idol-keepers

[[B2-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH

- Relationship: addressed — "I will sacrifice to you"
- Presence: PRESENT

- Role: the contrast — the ones who forsake their hesed
- Relationship: unnamed others, set against the praying "I"
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**

None: no place is named in this movement.

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[CB_0055-Vain-Idols]] — הַבְלֵי־שָׁוְא / worthless idols
- What it is: empty things held onto as gods
- Function in scene: what the contrasted ones keep — and what keeping costs them

[[CB_0011-Hesed]] — חַסְדָּם / their hesed
- What it is: hesed — loyal, covenant kindness (the loyal love between worshiper and God)
- Function in scene: what idol-keepers walk away from — the same hesed Ruth's story turns on, here forsaken

[[CB_0056-Salvation-Belongs-to-YHWH]] — יְשׁוּעָתָה / salvation
- What it is: rescue that is YHWH's to give
- Function in scene: the prayer's last word and the book's claim — salvation belongs to YHWH

**3D — Times**
- None: no time-setting; the vow looks forward, undated.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B8-Idol-Keepers]] Those who hold to [[CB_0055-Vain-Idols]] vain idols forsake their hesed. But I — with a voice of thanksgiving I will sacrifice to you; what I have vowed I will pay. [[CB_0056-Salvation-Belongs-to-YHWH]] Salvation belongs to [[B2-YHWH]] YHWH.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Closes the prayer outward and forward: the rescued man takes the worshiper's side against the idol-keepers, promises the thanksgiving sacrifice and the paid vow — the very acts the sailors did on deck — and hands the book its thesis before the mission resumes.

**Significant Absence**
The vow's content is never told — what he vowed stays unsaid. There is no promise to go to Nineveh, no word about the errand he fled; the prayer ends with salvation declared and the obedience question still open.
