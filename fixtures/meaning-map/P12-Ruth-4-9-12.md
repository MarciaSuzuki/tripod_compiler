# P12 — Ruth 4:9-12

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** You are witnesses this day: the names spoken and the gate's blessing
- **BCV:** Ruth 4:9-12
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

*(Versification note: Ruth's chapter and verse numbers are the same in English and Hebrew — no divergence in this pericope.)*

**Multi-level register tagging:**
Pericope-level INFORMAL_CASUAL — the narrator's framing of the two speeches ("and Boaz said," "and all the people said"). Scene 1, Boaz's attestation declaration (4:9–10), is FORMAL_OFFICIAL: the legal performative that completes the gate proceeding, spoken to the elders and all the people and bracketed by "you are witnesses today." Scene 2, the people's and elders' blessing (4:11–12), lifts to CEREMONIAL: a full communal blessing in set, weighty form, invoking YHWH by name and the matriarchs Rachel and Leah, and the house of Perez whom Tamar bore.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage is the book's record made public. Boaz turns from the sandal to the whole assembly and declares what he has done: you are witnesses today that I have bought all that was Elimelech's, and all that was Chilion's and Mahlon's, from Naomi's hand — and that I have bought Ruth the Moabite, the wife of Mahlon, to be my wife, to raise up the name of the dead upon his inheritance, so that the name of the dead is not cut off from his kindred and from the gate of his place; you are witnesses today. After eight verses of careful namelessness at the gate, the dead are named — Elimelech, Chilion, and Mahlon — and Ruth's husband, withheld since the first chapter, is spoken at last: she is the wife of Mahlon. The assembly answers as one: we are witnesses. Then they bless: may YHWH make the woman coming into your house like Rachel and Leah, who together built the house of Israel; do worthily in Ephrathah and call out a name in Bethlehem; and may your house be like the house of Perez, whom Tamar bore to Judah, from the seed YHWH gives you from this young woman. What weighs on the passage is the turn from law to blessing and from death to a house: the legal act that preserves a dead man's name becomes a prayer for a living line, and the divine name, silent through the whole proceeding, returns in the people's mouths. The question it leaves hanging is the one the blessing names: the seed, the house, the name yet to be born.

### 2.2 Context
This completes the gate scene the sandal sealed. Boaz's declaration gathers the whole legal substance into one public sentence: the field of Elimelech's line and the widow of Mahlon, bought together, so the dead man's name lives on his land. The blessing reaches back through the book and out past it. Rachel and Leah built the house of Israel as outsider-brides brought into the line — the model for Ruth the Moabite. Tamar bore Perez to Judah by a levirate-style claim pressed by a determined woman — the nearest precedent to Ruth's own story, and Perez stands at the head of the genealogy this book closes with. The divine name, which the narrator kept out of the entire legal proceeding (4:1–10), comes back exactly here, in the blessing — its absence through the law and its return at the blessing are both deliberate. Ruth and Naomi are still not present; the men at the gate decide and bless over them, as they have through the whole chapter.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
Formal, full, and rising. The declaration is dense and exact — every party named, every clause of the purchase laid out, the bracketing "you are witnesses" closing it like a seal. The assembly's answer is a single word, choral and quick. Then the blessing opens the passage outward and lifts it: three rich invocations, each reaching for a house and a name, each weightier than the last, ending on the seed YHWH will give. The pace moves from the tight legal recital to the broad, generous swing of the blessing — the gate, which has been doing careful procedural work, breaks into prayer.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
This passage seals the redemption publicly and turns it toward the future. It speaks the names the gate scene withheld — the three dead men, and Mahlon as Ruth's husband, closing the pairing the narrator held back from the start. It completes the raise-up-the-name formula the previous scene opened, binding the field, the widow, and the dead man's name into one witnessed act. It returns the divine name at the blessing after its structural silence through the law. It measures Ruth against the matriarchs and against Tamar, drawing the outsider-bride into the house of Israel and the Judah-line, and it opens the male-civic blessing that the women's blessing at the book's close will answer. To speak this passage is to keep the legal exactness of the declaration, the choral assent, the rising blessing, and the names — all the names — spoken aloud.

- Function in scene: the dead man's name secured against being cut off from his kindred and his gate — the formula's reach made legal fact

[[CB_0004-Moabite-Outsider]] — הַמּוֹאֲבִיָּה / "the Moabite"
- What it is: the foreigner-marker
- Function in scene: spoken at the moment of the public marriage-declaration — the outsider entered into the family record

[[CB_0006-Gate-Legal-Venue]] — מִשַּׁעַר מְקוֹמוֹ / "from the gate of his place"
- What it is: the gate as the legal-and-public venue where a name is kept
- Function in scene: the place the dead man's name is held in — to be cut off from it is to be erased from the town's record

**3D — Times**

[[TM_TODAY-Today]] — הַיּוֹם / "today"
- What it is: the day of the witnessing — twice named, opening and closing the declaration
- Function: the legal "today" the witnesses are bound to; the bracket of the performative speech

**3E — What Happens**
[[B13-Boaz]] Boaz says to [[B20-Elders]] the elders and to [[B21-All-the-People-at-the-Gate]] all the people: you are witnesses today that I have bought all that was [[B2-Elimelech]] Elimelech's, and all that was [[B5-Chilion]] Chilion's and [[B4-Mahlon]] Mahlon's, from the hand of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; and also [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth the Moabite, the wife of Mahlon, I have bought to be my wife, to raise up the name of the dead upon his inheritance, so that the name of the dead is not cut off from his kindred and from the gate of his place — you are witnesses today.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Makes the redemption public and complete: every party and holding named, the marriage declared, the dead man's name secured — and the names the gate withheld, including Mahlon as Ruth's husband, finally spoken.

**Significant Absence**
Ruth and Naomi are not present to hear themselves named; the declaration is made over them. The narrator does not record any change in Ruth's standing in her own voice — the marriage is announced as a thing done, not asked. And the divine name is still not spoken in this legal declaration; it waits for the blessing.

### Scene 2 — The assembly's witness and blessing (4:11–12)

**3A — Beings**

[[B21-All-the-People-at-the-Gate]] — כָּל־הָעָם אֲשֶׁר־בַּשַּׁעַר / "all the people who were at the gate"

- Relationship: the townspeople at the gate
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B20-Elders]] — הַזְּקֵנִים / "the elders"
- Role: the elders joined in the witness and the blessing
- Relationship: the seated court
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B10-YHWH]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- Role: the one the blessing invokes to make the woman fruitful and to give the seed
- Relationship: the God of Israel, named here for the first time since the proceeding began
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B13-Boaz]] — בֵּיתֶךָ / "your house"
- Role: the one the blessing is spoken over — his house, his name, his seed
- Relationship: the redeemer-bridegroom
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — הָאִשָּׁה הַבָּאָה אֶל־בֵּיתֶךָ … הַנַּעֲרָה הַזֹּאת / "the woman coming into your house … this young woman"
- Role: the bride the blessing measures against the matriarchs; the one the seed will come from
- Relationship: Ruth, named here only by her coming-in and her youth
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B22-Rachel-and-Leah]] — רָחֵל וְלֵאָה / Rachel and Leah
- Role: the matriarchs Ruth is likened to — who together built the house of Israel
- Relationship: the foundational mothers of the line, outsider-brides brought in
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B27-Genealogy-Figures]] — פֶּרֶץ / Perez
- Role: the lineage-house Boaz's house is likened to — the model of a line built through an unexpected mother
- Relationship: son of Judah and Tamar; head of the Judah-line the book closes with
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B23-Tamar]] — תָּמָר / Tamar
- Role: the mother who bore Perez to Judah — the nearest precedent to Ruth's own claim
- Relationship: the determined woman of the Judah-line; an outsider who pressed a levirate-style right
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B30-Judah]] — יְהוּדָה / Judah
- Role: the father of Perez by Tamar — the line Boaz's house is measured into
- Relationship: the patriarch of the tribe; the ancestor of the line
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**

[[PL7-Gate]] — בַּשַּׁעַר / "at the gate"
- Role: where the people stand to witness and bless
- Type: the town gate, the assembly's place
- Effect on scene: the civic venue gives the blessing its public weight

[[PL_ISRAEL-Israel]] — יִשְׂרָאֵל / Israel
- Role: the nation whose house Rachel and Leah built — what Ruth is measured into
- Type: the people-and-land of the covenant line
- Effect on scene: names the whole house the blessing draws the outsider-bride into

[[PL8-Ephrathah]] — אֶפְרָתָה / Ephrathah
- Role: the lineage-region the blessing names for Boaz to do worthily in
- Type: the older lineage-name of the Bethlehem area
- Effect on scene: paired with Bethlehem, it carries the weight of the line's name and place

[[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] — בֵּית לֶחֶם / Bethlehem
- Role: the town the blessing names for Boaz to call out a name in
- Type: the town the whole story returns to
- Effect on scene: where the name is to be made — the house of bread, no longer empty

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[CB_0008-Blessing]] — the people's blessing
- What it is: a communal blessing in set, weighty form
- Function in scene: the assembly's prayer over Boaz's house — three invocations, each reaching for a house and a name

[[CB_0010-House-of-Israel]] — בֵּית יִשְׂרָאֵל / "the house of [[PL_ISRAEL-Israel]] Israel"
- What it is: the corporate house of the nation — the line built by its mothers
- Function in scene: what Rachel and Leah built, and what Ruth is now measured into building

[[CB_0009-Divine-Name]] — יְהוָה / YHWH
- What it is: the divine name itself
- Function in scene: invoked twice in the blessing — its return after the legal silence is the structural point

[[CB_0049-Perez-as-Lineage-Founder]] — בֵּית פֶּרֶץ / "the house of Perez"
- What it is: Perez as the founder of the Judah-line through Tamar
- Function in scene: the model house the blessing measures Boaz's against; the head of the genealogy to come

**3D — Times**
- None: no distinct temporal frame; the blessing follows the witness directly, within the same assembly.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B21-All-the-People-at-the-Gate]] All the people at the gate and [[B20-Elders]] the elders say: we are witnesses. May [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH make [[B9-Ruth]] the woman coming into your house like [[B22-Rachel-and-Leah]] Rachel and Leah, who together built the house of Israel. Do worthily in [[PL8-Ephrathah]] Ephrathah and call out a name in [[PL1-Bethlehem-of-Judah]] Bethlehem. And may your house be like the house of [[B27-Genealogy-Figures]] Perez, whom [[B23-Tamar]] Tamar bore to [[B30-Judah]] Judah, from the seed that YHWH gives you from this young woman.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Answers the witness with a blessing that turns the law toward a future: Ruth measured to the matriarchs, the house measured to Perez's, and the seed asked of YHWH — the divine name returning at the prayer.

**Significant Absence**
Ruth, blessed by name of her role, is not there to receive it; the blessing is spoken to Boaz over her. The blessing names a son's line to come but no child yet; the seed is asked, not given. And no one at the gate names what the audience knows from the genealogy — that the name to be called out in Bethlehem runs to David.
