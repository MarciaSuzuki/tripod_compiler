# P08 — Ruth 3:1-5

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** Naomi's plan for rest; Ruth's total consent
- **BCV:** Ruth 3:1-5
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

*(Versification note: Ruth's chapter and verse numbers are the same in English and Hebrew — no divergence in this pericope.)*

**Multi-level register tagging:**
The whole passage sits in INFORMAL_CASUAL, the plain default for the biblical narrator's voice. Both scenes shift to INTIMATE at scene level: the entire pericope is the two women alone at home — a mother-in-law laying out a private, risky plan, and a daughter-in-law answering with one line of trust. There is no public moment and no ceremonial lift inside this talk. The narrator's own voice frames only the speech-openings at v.1 and v.5 and is never put in a consultative key.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage is one quiet exchange that turns the book from provision to permanence. Naomi opens with a question that is really a promise: shall I not seek for you a resting place (manoach — the secure home a married woman has), so that it may be well with you? She names the means in the next breath: Boaz, our kinsman, the man whose young women Ruth worked beside — and tonight he is winnowing barley at the threshing floor. Then the instructions, step by step: wash, anoint yourself, put on your garments, go down to the floor; stay unknown to the man until he has finished eating and drinking; mark the place where he lies; go in, uncover the place of his feet, and lie down. The plan ends not with its goal but with a handoff: he will tell you what to do. Ruth answers with everything at once: all that you say I will do. What weighs on the passage is what is not said — the plan stops at the man's feet and never names marriage, and the risk to a foreign widow alone at night at the floor is left entirely unspoken. The question it leaves hanging is what will happen in the dark between the lying down and the telling.

### 2.2 Context
This follows straight on the harvests' end: the gleaning that fed the two women is over, and provision must now become something lasting. Naomi's question picks up her own old blessing-wish — at 1:9 she asked YHWH to grant each daughter-in-law rest (menucha) in the house of a husband; here she stops wishing and starts seeking that rest herself. The means is what P07 recognized: Boaz is near kin (2:20), named here by the softer family word — "our kinsman" (moda) — while the legal word, redeemer, stays unspoken. Winnowing brings the owner to the threshing floor overnight with the grain, which is what gives the plan its night, its place, and its danger. The audience still carries Boaz's open blessing from 2:12 — may YHWH reward her under whose wings she took refuge — without knowing yet that the plan is walking Ruth toward the man who spoke it.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
Hushed and deliberate. Naomi's speech builds in small steady steps — a question, a name, a time and place, then command after command — each one plain, none explained. The care in the instructions (bathe, anoint, dress) runs right beside their risk (alone, at night, uncovering, lying down), and the speech holds both without naming either. The pace is a single accumulating instruction; then the passage stops short on Ruth's one-line answer, and the stillness after it is the door the next pericope walks through.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
This passage turns the rest-wish of 1:9 into a plan with a name, a night, and a place — the hinge from the harvest chapters to the threshing-floor night. It opens the floor scene that the next two pericopes live inside. It hands the initiative the book has been tracking — Naomi's word — toward Ruth's act, with Boaz's word waiting at the end ("he will tell you what to do"). It opens the assent-pattern: Ruth's "all that you say I will do" will come back in Boaz's own mouth at 3:11. And it keeps the deepest thing unsaid: the word redeemer, spoken once at 2:20, waits to be spoken next by Ruth herself in the dark.

## 3. Level 2 — Scenes / Episodes

### Scene 1 — The plan spoken (3:1–4)

**3A — Beings**

[[B3-Naomi]] — נָעֳמִי חֲמוֹתָהּ / Naomi her mother-in-law
- Role: the planner — she names the goal, the man, the night, and every step
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth; widow of [[B2-Elimelech]] Elimelech; kinswoman by marriage to [[B13-Boaz]] Boaz
- Referential form: the narrator gives both the name and the kinship-word together — "Naomi her mother-in-law"
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B9-Ruth]] — בִּתִּי / "my daughter"
- Role: the one the rest is sought for; the hearer of the instructions
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Referential form: addressed only as "my daughter"; her name is not spoken in this scene
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B13-Boaz]] — בֹּעַז מֹדַעְתָּנוּ / "Boaz our kinsman"
- Role: the means of the plan — the kinsman winnowing at the floor tonight; the man Ruth must not be known to until the right moment
- Relationship: kinsman of the household of [[B2-Elimelech]] Elimelech; the field-owner Ruth gleaned beside all season
- Referential form: "our kinsman" (moda — the family word, not the legal one) at v.2; then only "the man" at v.3
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B16-Young-Women]] — נַעֲרוֹתָיו / "his young women"
- Role: the workers Ruth was beside all season — the tie Naomi names between Ruth and Boaz
- Relationship: Boaz's female field-workers
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**

[[PL_NAOMIS_DWELLING-Naomis-Dwelling-in-Bethlehem]] — implied
- Role: the home where the plan is spoken
- Type: a private household space, not named in the text
- Effect on scene: the closed door the whole plan is laid behind

[[PL6-Threshing-Floor]] — הַגֹּרֶן / the threshing floor
- Role: where the plan points — Boaz winnows there tonight; Ruth is to go down to it
- Type: the open working-floor where grain is winnowed, and where the owner sleeps by his grain
- Effect on scene: the named destination that gives the plan its night, its place, and its risk

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[CB_0014-Rest-Menucha]] — מָנוֹחַ / "a resting place"
- What it is: rest — the secure household-place a married woman has; the settled life
- Function in scene: what Naomi sets out to seek for Ruth — the goal the whole plan serves
- Cross-ref: picks up Naomi's own wish at 1:9, "rest, each in the house of her husband" — the wish becomes a plan here

[[O2-Barley]] — הַשְּׂעֹרִים / the barley
- What it is: the harvested grain being winnowed
- Function in scene: what brings Boaz to the floor tonight — the winnowing of the season's grain

[[O13-Your-Garments]] — שִׂמְלֹתַיִךְ / "your garments"
- What it is: Ruth's outer clothing, put on after washing and anointing
- Function in scene: the third step of the preparation — dressed for being seen, after a season dressed for gleaning

[[CB_0042-Uncover-Feet-Margelot]] — מַרְגְּלֹתָיו / "the place of his feet"
- What it is: the place where a sleeper's feet lie; to uncover it is to fold the covering back from his feet
- Function in scene: the plan's boldest instruction — the act that will wake the man to a woman lying there
- Cross-ref: the word recurs through the night scenes (3:4, 7, 8, 14); what exactly the gesture asks is left for the next pericope to carry

**3D — Times**
- None: the talk itself sits in an unmarked moment at home; "tonight" inside the plan points at the coming winnowing-night, not at this scene's own frame.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B3-Naomi]] Naomi her mother-in-law speaks to [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth: my daughter, shall I not seek a resting place for you, that it may be well with you? And now — is not [[B13-Boaz]] Boaz our kinsman, with whose young women you were? Behold, he is winnowing the barley at the [[PL6-Threshing-Floor]] threshing floor tonight. Wash, anoint yourself, put your garments on you, and go down to the floor. Do not be known to the man until he has finished eating and drinking. When he lies down, know the place where he lies; go in, uncover the place of his feet, and lie down. And he — he will tell you what you shall do.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Turns the rest-wish into a worked plan: goal, man, night, place, and step-by-step instructions, ending with the next word handed to Boaz.

**Significant Absence**
The plan never names its goal outright — marriage is not said, and what should happen after the lying down is left entirely to "he will tell you what you shall do." The word redeemer, spoken at 2:20, is not spoken here — Boaz is named by the softer family word, "our kinsman." And the risk — a woman alone at night at the floor — is never acknowledged.

### Scene 2 — The consent (3:5)

**3A — Beings**

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: the one who consents — completely, and without one question
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B3-Naomi]] — אֵלֶיהָ / "to her"
- Role: the one whose word is accepted whole
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: PRESENT

**3B — Places**

[[PL_NAOMIS_DWELLING-Naomis-Dwelling-in-Bethlehem]] — implied
- Role: the same room, the same talk
- Type: a private household space, not named in the text
- Effect on scene: holds the consent inside the same privacy as the plan

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

None: no persistent objects in this scene.

**3D — Times**
- None: continuous with the talk of Scene 1.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B9-Ruth]] Ruth says to her: all that you say I will do.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Closes the exchange with total assent — one line, no questions — and sends the plan toward the floor.

**Significant Absence**
Ruth asks nothing — not the goal, not the meaning of the uncovering, not what to do if the man is angry. The narrator records no hesitation between hearing the plan and accepting all of it.
