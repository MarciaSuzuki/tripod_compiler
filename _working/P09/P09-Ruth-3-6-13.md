# P09 — Ruth 3:6-13

## 1. Metadata
- **Pericope title:** The threshing-floor night: the wing asked for, the redeemer named, the oath
- **BCV:** Ruth 3:6-13
- **Genre Group:** NARRATIVE
- **Genre:** HISTORICAL_NARRATIVE
- **Register:** INFORMAL_CASUAL

*(Versification note: Ruth's chapter and verse numbers are the same in English and Hebrew — no divergence in this pericope.)*

**Multi-level register tagging:**
The whole passage sits in INFORMAL_CASUAL. Scenes 2 and 3 shift to INTIMATE: a man and a woman speaking in whispers in the dark, midnight, alone at the floor — the most private exchange in the book. One moment inside it lifts to CEREMONIAL: Boaz's blessing at v.10 ("blessed are you of YHWH, my daughter") takes the set, weighty blessing form. The oath at v.13 ("as YHWH lives") stays INTIMATE rather than ceremonial, on the P03 precedent for Ruth's own oath: its weight is carried by the fixed formula and its figure, not by a grander way of speaking. Scene 1 is the narrator's plain telling.

## 2. Level 1 — Whole-Passage Movement

### 2.1 Prose Arc / Shape / Argument / Burden / Concern
The passage walks Naomi's plan into the dark and out the other side, changed. Ruth goes down to the floor and does all her mother-in-law commanded. Boaz eats and drinks, his heart is good, and he lies down at the end of the grain heap; she comes softly, uncovers the place of his feet, and lies down. At half of the night the man trembles, twists — and there is a woman lying at his feet. He asks who she is. She answers with her name and more than the plan gave her: I am Ruth your servant — spread your wing over your servant, for you are a redeemer. The plan had said he would tell her what to do; instead she tells him. Boaz answers with a blessing — blessed are you of YHWH, my daughter — and calls this her last hesed (loyal, covenant kindness), better than the first: she has not gone after the young men, poor or rich. He quiets her fear, gives back her own words from the plan's first scene — all that you say I will do for you — and gives the reason: the whole gate of my people knows you are a woman of worth. Then the turn no one planned for: truly I am a redeemer — but there is a redeemer nearer than I. He sets the morning's protocol — if he redeems you, good, let him redeem; if he does not want to, I will redeem you myself — and seals it with the oath: as YHWH lives. Lie down until the morning. What weighs on the passage is how much hangs in the dark between two careful people — desire, law, honor, and a third man's prior claim — and the question it leaves hanging is which redeemer the morning will give her.

### 2.2 Context
This is the execution of P08's plan, and its overturning. Naomi's instructions run out exactly where she said they would — "he will tell you what you shall do" — and at that point Ruth speaks instead, asking for the very thing Boaz once asked YHWH to give her: at 2:12 he blessed her under the wings (kanaf) of the God of Israel; at 3:9 she says, spread your wing (kanaf) over your servant. The word redeemer, withheld through the plan (P08 called him only "our kinsman"), is spoken at last — by Ruth, in the dark, as a claim. Boaz's "last hesed better than the first" reaches back through the book's hesed-thread (1:8; 2:20): the first hesed was her loyalty to the dead and to Naomi; the last is choosing the household's redeemer over the young men. His "woman of worth" (eshet chayil) completes the pair the narrator opened at 2:1, where Boaz entered as a man of worth (ish gibbor chayil). And his disclosure — a redeemer nearer than I — turns 2:20's "one of our redeemers" from comfort into a legal queue, and hands the story to the gate.

### 2.3 Emotion / Tone / Pace
The first scene is all quiet motion — eating, drinking, a good heart, lying down, soft footsteps, an uncovering, a lying down — told plainly and fast. The midnight startle snaps the stillness: a tremble, a twist, a woman where no one should be. Then the pace stops moving and the talk takes over, hushed and high-stakes: her one sentence asking for everything; his answer unrolling slowly — blessing, praise, reassurance, complication, oath. The held breath of the passage is the gap between "you are a redeemer" and "there is a redeemer nearer than I." It ends with the strange calm of a settled protocol: lie down until the morning.

### 2.4 Communicative Function
This passage is the hinge of the book: the wing-image opened at 2:12 closes here as a marriage petition; the assent-pattern opened at 3:5 comes back in Boaz's mouth; the handoff-pattern reverses — the plan said he would tell her, and she tells him. It puts the word redeemer in Ruth's mouth for the first time and immediately complicates it with the nearer redeemer, opening the legal question the gate scene will resolve. It fixes Ruth's public standing — a woman of worth, known at the gate — matching her to Boaz's own introduction. It binds the night's promise under the chai-YHWH oath. To speak this passage is to keep the whispers, the weight of the formulas inside them, and the unresolved queue of redeemers the morning must sort.

## 3. Level 2 — Scenes / Episodes

### Scene 1 — Down to the floor; the uncovering (3:6–7)

**3A — Beings**

[[B9-Ruth]] — רוּת / Ruth
- Role: the one who goes down, does all as commanded, comes softly, uncovers, and lies down
- Relationship: Moabite daughter-in-law of [[B3-Naomi]] Naomi; acting on her plan
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B13-Boaz]] — בֹּעַז / Boaz
- Role: the one who eats and drinks, whose heart is good, and who lies down by the grain
- Relationship: the kinsman of P08's plan; he does not yet know she is there
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B3-Naomi]] — חֲמוֹתָהּ / "her mother-in-law"
- Role: the absent planner whose command Ruth carries out
- Relationship: mother-in-law of [[B9-Ruth]] Ruth
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**

[[PL6-Threshing-Floor]] — הַגֹּרֶן / the threshing floor
- Role: the scene of the whole night
- Type: the open working-floor; the owner sleeps by his grain at winnowing time
- Effect on scene: the place the plan named, now entered

קְצֵה הָעֲרֵמָה / "the end of the grain heap"
- Role: where Boaz lies down
- Type: the edge of the piled grain on the floor
- Effect on scene: fixes the place Ruth must find in the dark

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[CB_0042-Uncover-Feet-Margelot]] — מַרְגְּלֹתָיו / "the place of his feet"
- What it is: the place where a sleeper's feet lie; to uncover it is to fold the covering back from his feet
- Function in scene: the act the plan ordered, now done — softly, while he sleeps

**3D — Times**
- None as a distinct frame: the scene runs from the evening meal into the night; the night itself is marked at the next scene's startle.

**3E — What Happens**
[[B9-Ruth]] Ruth goes down to the [[PL6-Threshing-Floor]] threshing floor and does according to all that her mother-in-law commanded her. [[B13-Boaz]] Boaz eats and drinks, and his heart is good; he comes to lie down at the end of the grain heap. She comes softly, uncovers the place of his feet, and lies down.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Executes the plan to the letter and sets the night's stage: the man asleep by his grain, the woman at his feet, and nothing yet said.

**Significant Absence**
The narrator does not say what Ruth intends beyond the plan, and does not name the risk. No word is spoken in the whole scene; the night holds its breath.

### Scene 2 — Midnight: the startle, the question, the request (3:8–9)

**3A — Beings**

[[B13-Boaz]] — הָאִישׁ / "the man"

- Referential form: "a woman" to the startled man; then by her own mouth, "Ruth your servant" — amah, the marriageable-servant word, a step up from her shifchah of 2:13
- Presence: PRESENT

[[B18-The-Redeemer]] — גֹאֵל / "a redeemer"
- Role: the role Ruth names Boaz into — the ground of her request
- Relationship: the near-kinsman duty of [[B2-Elimelech]] Elimelech's line; [[B13-Boaz]] Boaz holds it
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**

[[PL6-Threshing-Floor]] — implied
- Role: the dark floor where the exchange happens
- Type: the open working-floor at midnight
- Effect on scene: total privacy — no witness to what is asked or answered

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[CB_0037-Refuge-Under-Wings-Kanaph]] — כְנָפֶךָ / "your wing"
- What it is: the wing as the picture of protective covering — shelter taken under it
- Function in scene: Ruth asks Boaz to spread his wing over her — to become the shelter himself
- Cross-ref: the same word Boaz used at 2:12, blessing her under YHWH's wings; the pair closes here as a petition

[[CB_0001-Kinsman-Redeemer]] — גֹאֵל / "a redeemer"
- What it is: the kinsman-redeemer — a near relative bound to step in for a dead clansman's family, land, and name
- Function in scene: the word the plan withheld, spoken at last — by Ruth, as the reason for her request
- Cross-ref: named "one of our redeemers" at 2:20; the legal word enters Ruth's own mouth here

**3D — Times**

בַּחֲצִי הַלַּיְלָה / "at half of the night"
- What it is: midnight — the middle of the night
- Function: the time-anchor of the startle; the request is made in the deepest dark

**3E — What Happens**
At half of the night [[B13-Boaz]] the man trembles and twists — and behold, a woman lying at the place of his feet. He says: who are you? [[B9-Ruth]] She says: I am Ruth your servant. Spread your wing over your servant, for you are a redeemer.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
The turn of the night: the plan's script runs out and Ruth speaks past it — name, petition, and the redeemer-claim, in one breath in the dark.

**Significant Absence**
Ruth does not wait for the man to tell her what to do, though that is what the plan promised; the narrator does not mark the breach — her speech simply takes the plan's place. What the uncovering and the lying-down mean beyond themselves is never said; the text keeps the night's modesty. And no word of love or desire is spoken by either of them.

**3A — Beings**

[[B13-Boaz]] — בֹּעַז / Boaz

- Relationship: the God under whose wing Boaz once placed her; now invoked over his own pledge
- Presence: REFERENCED

- Role: the unnamed prior claim — the complication the morning must resolve
- Relationship: a kinsman of [[B2-Elimelech]] Elimelech's line, nearer in the legal queue than Boaz
- Referential form: unnamed here — only "a redeemer nearer than I"
- Presence: REFERENCED

[[B21-All-the-People-at-the-Gate]] — כָּל־שַׁעַר עַמִּי / "all the gate of my people"
- Role: the public whose knowledge of Ruth's worth Boaz cites
- Relationship: the town community at its gate — the same gate the legal scene will use
- Presence: REFERENCED

הַבַּחוּרִים / "the young men"
- Role: the ones Ruth did not go after — the choice her hesed is measured by
- Relationship: the young men of marrying age, poor or rich; none of them chosen
- Presence: REFERENCED

**3B — Places**

[[PL6-Threshing-Floor]] — implied
- Role: the dark floor holding the whole answer
- Type: the open working-floor at night
- Effect on scene: the privacy that lets the law be spoken gently

[[PL7-Gate]] — שַׁעַר / "the gate"
- Role: the place Boaz's praise points to — where his people's knowledge lives
- Type: the town gate, the public meeting-and-law place
- Effect on scene: reaches ahead to where the queue of redeemers will be sorted

**3C — Objects and Concepts**

[[CB_0008-Blessing]] — בְּרוּכָה אַתְּ לַיהוָה / "blessed are you of YHWH"
- What it is: a blessing in set, weighty form
- Function in scene: Boaz's first word back — the night's request answered first with blessing; lifts to a ceremonial key

[[CB_0011-Hesed]] — חַסְדֵּךְ / "your hesed"
- What it is: hesed — loyal, covenant kindness that holds people to one another beyond bare duty
- Function in scene: how Boaz names what Ruth has just done — her last hesed, better than the first
- Cross-ref: the thread runs 1:8 and 2:20; here it is measured — the first her loyalty to the dead and to Naomi, the last her choice of the household's redeemer over the young men

[[CB_0032-Chayil]] — אֵשֶׁת חַיִל / "a woman of worth"
- What it is: chayil — substance, standing, capability, worth
- Function in scene: Ruth's public standing in Boaz's mouth — what all the gate of his people knows
- Cross-ref: completes the pair with Boaz's own introduction at 2:1, a man of worth

[[CB_0006-Gate-Legal-Venue]] — שַׁעַר עַמִּי / "the gate of my people"
- What it is: the gate as the town's public and legal venue
- Function in scene: where Ruth's worth is known — and where, unspoken yet, the redeemer-queue will be sorted

[[CB_0001-Kinsman-Redeemer]] — גֹאֵל / "redeemer"
- What it is: the kinsman-redeemer duty
- Function in scene: Boaz owns the role — truly I am a redeemer — and discloses the nearer one; the redeem-word then runs through the morning protocol, redeem upon redeem
- Cross-ref: the queue this scene opens is resolved at the gate (4:1–8)

**3D — Times**

הַלַּיְלָה … בַבֹּקֶר / "the night … in the morning"
- What it is: the night that holds them, and the morning that will decide
- Function: the protocol's two times — lodge the night; the morning brings the nearer redeemer's answer

**3E — What Happens**
[[B13-Boaz]] Boaz says: blessed are you of [[B10-YHWH]] YHWH, my daughter. You have made your last hesed better than the first — not going after the young men, whether poor or rich. And now, my daughter, do not fear; all that you say I will do for you, for all the gate of my people knows that you are a woman of worth. And now — truly I am a redeemer; but there is also a redeemer nearer than I. Lodge the night; in the morning, if he redeems you — good, let him redeem; and if he does not want to redeem you, then I will redeem you myself — as YHWH lives. Lie down until the morning.

**3F — Communicative Purpose**
Answers the night's petition with everything at once — blessing, honor, pledge, and law: Ruth is praised and promised, the nearer redeemer is disclosed, and the whole matter is bound under oath and handed to the morning.

**Significant Absence**
Boaz does not say yes to the marriage itself — his pledge is to the redeeming, with the queue honored first; what he wants is never said apart from what is right. The nearer redeemer is not named. And nothing improper happens in the dark: the narrator marks no touch beyond the uncovering, and the night passes in words.
